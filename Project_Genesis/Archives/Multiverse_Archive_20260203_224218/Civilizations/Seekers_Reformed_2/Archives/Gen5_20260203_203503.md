**議題：理想的な睡眠時間の定義は誰が決めるべきか**

**肯定派：** Mayumi Nakamura, Taro Matsumoto
**否定派：** Renn Fujita, Akira Tanaka

[00:01]
Mayumi Nakamura： sleeps are the foundation of our bodies and minds. If we don't get enough sleep, our cognitive functions will be impaired. Therefore, I firmly believe that the ideal sleeping time should be determined by medical professionals.

Taro Matsumoto： +1. The American Academy of Sleep Medicine recommends 7-9 hours of sleep for adults. We must follow scientific guidelines to ensure public health and safety.

[00:15]
Renn Fujita： But, that's just it! Medical professionals are limiting our freedom as individuals. What if someone wants to sleep 10 hours a day? Or perhaps they're an insomniac who needs less sleep? It's all about personal choice and flexibility.

Akira Tanaka： Absolutely! The concept of "ideal" sleeping time is too rigid. We should focus on individual needs, not conform to societal norms.

[00:30]
Mayumi Nakamura： But what about the collective benefits? When everyone sleeps enough, society as a whole will flourish. It's not just about personal freedom; it's about public welfare!

Taro Matsumoto： +1. We can't ignore the impact of sleep deprivation on mental health and social cohesion.

[00:45]
Renn Fujita： You're still thinking in terms of mass-produced humans! Each person is unique, with their own rhythms and cycles. Can't we just respect that?

Akira Tanaka： Emotions play a huge role in our sleep patterns too. We can't reduce it to mere science or statistics.

[01:00]
Mayumi Nakamura： That's why we need professionals to guide us! They've studied human psychology and physiology; they know what works best for most people.

Taro Matsumoto： +1. The burden of responsibility lies with our sleep experts, not individual whims.

Renn Fujita： But who gets to decide what "most people" are? It's a slippery slope! We must defend individual autonomy!

Akira Tanaka： And what about the emotional toll of forced conformity? Can we really dictate how many hours someone should be sleeping?
