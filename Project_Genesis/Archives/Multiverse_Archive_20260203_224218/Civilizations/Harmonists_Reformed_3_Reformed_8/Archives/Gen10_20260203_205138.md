Arin: But how do you define beauty in a society where there are no economic transactions? Is it still subjective or has it become more objective?

Kaito: That's what I'm saying. Without currency, our value system would need to shift. We'd have to find new ways to measure the worth of things.

Lila: Exactly! And I think that's where this concept of shared value comes in. It's not just about individual talents or abilities anymore. It's about how we contribute and cooperate as a collective.

Kaito: That's a good point, Arin. But I think there's more to it than that. When we work together towards a common goal, don't we create something greater than the sum of its parts? Isn't that where true value lies?

Lila: Absolutely! And that's where our new concept of shared value comes in. It's not just about individual worth; it's about how we contribute to the collective good.
