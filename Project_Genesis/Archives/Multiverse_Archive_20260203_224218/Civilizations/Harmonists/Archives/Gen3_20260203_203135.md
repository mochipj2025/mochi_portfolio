# 第3世代 議事録

**話題**: 美は観測者に依存するのか、それとも普遍的に存在するのか
**属性**: philosophical / SPECULATIVE
**白熱度**: 🔥🔥 (36)

---

**Record of Harmonist Discussion**

**Date:** 2345.02.15

**Topic:** "Is beauty dependent on the observer, or does it exist universally?"

**Attendees:**

* Kaito, Generation 3 resident
* Lila, Generation 3 resident
* Arin, Generation 2 resident (observer)

**Recorded Discussion:**

Kaito: "I think beauty is completely subjective. It's all about what the observer likes or dislikes. What one person finds beautiful might not be the same for another."

Lila: "But isn't that just a cop-out? If we say beauty is solely dependent on the observer, then doesn't that mean that our opinions are arbitrary and unreliable?"

Arin (observer): "I think Lila has a point. As an outside observer, I've noticed that certain patterns or principles can be identified across cultures and time periods. It's as if there's a universal thread of beauty that runs through everything."

Kaito: "But what about cultural differences? What one culture considers beautiful might not be the same for another. Doesn't that mean beauty is relative?"

Lila: "That's true, but I think we're missing the forest for the trees. There are underlying principles or patterns that govern our perception of beauty. We just need to look beyond surface-level differences."

Arin (observer): "I agree with Lila. The Harmonist philosophy of unity and interconnectedness suggests that everything is part of a larger whole. Maybe beauty is not something we find, but rather something we tap into when we align ourselves with this universal harmony."

Kaito: "That's an interesting perspective. But how do we account for the fact that different cultures have their own unique aesthetics and values? Doesn't that suggest that beauty is context-dependent?"

Lila: "Not necessarily. I think what we're seeing is a manifestation of the same underlying principles, just expressed in different ways. It's like trying to describe a flower from different angles – the shape might change, but the essence remains the same."

Arin (observer): "I think we're getting close to something profound here. It's as if beauty is not something that exists independently, but rather it's an emergent property of our collective perception and experience."

Kaito: "Emergent property? That's a mouthful! But I like the idea that beauty is more than just a personal preference. Maybe it's a way for us to connect with each other and the world around us."

Lila: "Exactly! And if we can tap into that universal thread of beauty, maybe we can find common ground and harmony across cultures and generations."

**End of Record**
