**属性**: mundane / SERIOUS

**10th Generation: Exciting Debate**

**Theme:** "The Underlying Primitive Comfort in Queuing"

**Record of the Discussion:**

[Scene 1: Positive Aesthetic - Taro Matsumoto]

Matsumoto: "When we queue up, I think there's a deep-seated comfort that arises from being part of a collective experience. It's almost primal, like our ancestors waiting for food or shelter. This shared waiting becomes a form of social bonding."

[Scene 2: Negative Aesthetic - Akira Tanaka]

Tanaka: "I disagree. Queuing is just a necessary evil in modern society. We're not animals seeking sustenance; we're individuals with complex emotions and desires. There's no comfort in being shuffled along like cattle."

[Scene 3: Mediator - Mayumi Nakamura]

Nakamura: "Let's explore this idea further. Taro, can you elaborate on what you mean by 'primitive comfort'?"

Matsumoto: "For me, it's about tapping into our shared humanity. When we queue, we're reminded that we're all in this together, facing the same challenges and uncertainties. It's a fleeting sense of community."

[Scene 4: Counterpoint - Renn Fujita]

Fujita: "But isn't queuing just a sign of societal inefficiency? We're not being efficient or productive when we're waiting in line. The comfort Matsumoto speaks of is just an illusion born from boredom and stagnation."

[Scene 5: Emotional Exchange - Taro Matsumoto & Akira Tanaka]

Matsumoto: "You're missing the point, Renn! Queuing isn't just about efficiency; it's about social interaction. We learn to wait patiently alongside others, sharing a moment of humanity in an increasingly isolated world."

Tanaka: "That's sentimental nonsense, Taro! The only thing we're learning is how to waste our time. We should be pushing for better systems and services, not romanticizing the inefficiencies of queuing!"

[Scene 6: Conclusion - Mayumi Nakamura]

Nakamura: "It seems we've reached a deadlock. While both perspectives have merit, perhaps the truth lies in finding a balance between efficiency and social bonding. Queuing can be a chance for us to connect with others on a primal level, even if we don't always acknowledge it."

[End of Recording]
