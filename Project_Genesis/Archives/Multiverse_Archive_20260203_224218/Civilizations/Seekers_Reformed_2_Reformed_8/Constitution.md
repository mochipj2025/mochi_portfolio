After analyzing the dialogue, I've identified two opposing ideas:

**Relativist Aesthetic vs Essentialist Aesthetic**

* Relativist Aesthetic (Taro Matsumoto): Beauty is dependent on the observer's perspective and cultural context. The beauty of something is determined by how we perceive it.
* Essentialist Aesthetic (Akira Tanaka & Renn Fujita): There is an objective, universal beauty that exists independently of human perception or cultural influence. Beauty can be appreciated across cultures and contexts.

These two perspectives seem to be at the heart of the discussion, with Mayumi Nakamura serving as a mediator, seeking to understand the nuances between these opposing views. 🤝
