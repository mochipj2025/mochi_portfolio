# 新しい概念 (Neurodiverse Individuality)

## 定義
Neurodiverse Individuality refers to the inherent variability in human experiences, behaviors, and needs, which cannot be reduced to a single, universally applicable standard or formula.

## 意義
This concept acknowledges that individual differences in cognition, personality, and behavior are not exceptions to the rule, but rather an integral part of the human experience. It recognizes that people's unique characteristics, strengths, and challenges are shaped by their distinct neurobiological profiles, environmental influences, and life experiences. As such, Neurodiverse Individuality emphasizes the importance of embracing and celebrating diversity, rather than striving for a one-size-fits-all approach.

In the context of sleep patterns, Neurodiverse Individuality suggests that there is no single "right" answer to the question of how much sleep is ideal. Instead, it acknowledges that people's sleep needs are as varied as their personalities, interests, and abilities. This concept encourages individuals to focus on what works best for them, rather than trying to conform to external expectations or norms. Ultimately, Neurodiverse Individuality promotes a culture of acceptance, inclusivity, and self-awareness, where diversity is valued and celebrated.
