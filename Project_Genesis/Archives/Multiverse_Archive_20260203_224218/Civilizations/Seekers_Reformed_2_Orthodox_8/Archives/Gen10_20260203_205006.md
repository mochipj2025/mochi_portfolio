# 第10世代 議事録

**属性**: social / SPECULATIVE
**白熱度**: 🔥🔥 (45)

---

**Seekers Reformed 2 Orthodox 8: Notes on the Discussion**

**Theme:** "The Shape of Happiness that Results from Prohibiting Private Property"

**Moderator:** Mayumi Nakamura

**Participants:** Taro Matsumoto, Akira Tanaka, Renn Fujita

**Recorded Discourse:**

[Scene 1]

Mayumi: So, let's start with the idea that private property is prohibited. How do you think it would affect our daily lives?

Taro (Relativist Aesthetic): Well, I think it would lead to a more equal distribution of resources and a greater emphasis on community cooperation. People would prioritize sharing and mutual support over individual ownership.

Akira & Renn (Essentialist Aesthetic): But that's exactly the problem! Without private property, people wouldn't have the freedom to pursue their own interests or creative endeavors. It would stifle innovation and progress.

Mayumi: That's an interesting point. Akira, can you elaborate on how this relates to beauty?

Akira: Well, without private property, people might not be motivated to create beautiful works of art or music, as they wouldn't have the means to do so. Beauty is often tied to individual expression and ownership.

Taro: But isn't that just a narrow view of what constitutes beauty? Can't we find beauty in collective creations or community-driven projects?

Renn: I think Akira's point is valid, though. Without private property, people might not have the same sense of pride or accomplishment when it comes to their creative endeavors.

Mayumi: That's a great point. Renn, can you take us further into this idea? How would prohibiting private property affect our relationships with one another?

Renn: I think it would lead to more emphasis on interpersonal connections and community building. People would focus on nurturing relationships rather than accumulating wealth or material possessions.

Taro: That sounds incredibly freeing! We'd prioritize shared experiences and collective growth over individual gain.

Akira: But wouldn't that also mean we'd lose our sense of autonomy and personal freedom?

Mayumi: Ah, another great point! It seems like prohibiting private property would have far-reaching implications for our values and priorities. Let's continue exploring this idea...
