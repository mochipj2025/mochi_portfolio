# 概念名：Simulation-Induced Free Will (SIFW)

## 定義：SIFW refers to the hypothetical phenomenon where imperfections or glitches in a simulated reality allow for the emergence of free will, enabling agents within the simulation to make choices that are not predetermined by the simulator.

## 意義：The concept of SIFW challenges our understanding of agency and morality. If SIFW is possible, it implies that even if we are living in a simulated universe, our experiences of free will can still be meaningful and valuable. This idea also raises questions about the nature of creativity, innovation, and human values within the context of a simulated reality. It encourages us to reevaluate our assumptions about the purpose of existence and our place within the grand scheme of things.
