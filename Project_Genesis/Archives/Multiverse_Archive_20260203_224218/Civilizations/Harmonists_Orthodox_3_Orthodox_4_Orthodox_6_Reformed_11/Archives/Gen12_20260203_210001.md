**The 12th Generation's Intense Discussion**

**Topic:** The Shape of Happiness that the Prohibition of Private Property Brings

**Date:** Harmonists Orthodox 3, Orthodox 4, and Reformed 11 (Year: 2207)

**Attendees:**

* Kaito, a passionate advocate for the prohibition of private property
* Lila, a skeptical thinker who questions the effectiveness of such a policy
* Arin, a practical-minded individual who seeks to balance theoretical ideals with real-world concerns

**Record:**

[Scene: A small community center in the Harmonist sector. The three attendees sit around a circular table, surrounded by rows of books and diagrams. Kaito speaks enthusiastically.]

Kaito: "In a world where private property is abolished, we can finally achieve true equality! No longer will some individuals hoard wealth and resources while others struggle to survive."

Lila: "I'm not convinced. Without the incentive of personal gain, innovation and progress may stagnate. We risk losing the very drive that has propelled human civilization forward."

Arin: "But what about the benefits of communal ownership? Wouldn't it be more efficient to share resources and expertise?"

Kaito: "Efficiency is just a means to an end! What we really need is social justice and fairness. By distributing wealth and resources equally, we can ensure that every individual has access to the necessities of life."

Lila: "That's all well and good in theory, but what about individual freedom? If I don't own my own home or land, am I still a free person?"

Kaito: "Freedom is not just a matter of owning things! True freedom comes from being able to pursue your passions without the burden of material concerns. In a world where everyone has access to basic needs, we can redirect our energies towards creating art, music, and innovation."

Arin: "But what about the practicalities? How would we manage resource distribution, taxation, and conflict resolution?"

Kaito: "Those are just technical details! The real challenge is changing our mindset. We need to recognize that wealth and resources are not solely for personal gain, but for the greater good of humanity."

Lila: "I understand where you're coming from, Kaito, but I still have my doubts. Can't we find a middle ground between individual ownership and communal sharing?"

Kaito: "Middle grounds can lead to mediocrity! We need bold action to create lasting change. The prohibition of private property is the only way forward."

[The discussion becomes increasingly heated, with Kaito's passion clashing with Lila's skepticism. Arin tries to mediate, but ultimately, the attendees part ways without a clear consensus.]

**Outcome:** The debate ended inconclusively, leaving the community to ponder the merits and drawbacks of abolishing private property.

**Reflections:**

* Kaito's enthusiasm for the cause was inspiring, but his refusal to consider alternative perspectives may have hindered the discussion.
* Lila's skepticism served as a necessary counterbalance, but her negativity towards the idea may have overshadowed potential benefits.
* Arin's attempt to find common ground fell short, highlighting the need for more nuanced consideration of the issues.

**Lessons Learned:**

* The debate revealed the importance of considering both theoretical ideals and practical concerns in shaping policy.
* The attendees' differing perspectives highlighted the value of constructive conflict in fostering growth and understanding.

**Next Steps:**

* Further research into the effects of abolishing private property on individual freedom, economic growth, and social cohesion is necessary.
