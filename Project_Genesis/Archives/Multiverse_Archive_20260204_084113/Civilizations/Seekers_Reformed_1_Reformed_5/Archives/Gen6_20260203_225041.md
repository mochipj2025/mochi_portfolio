第6世代の激しい討論

Seekers Reformed 1-5, gather round! Today, we shall discuss the age-old question: "Is beauty subject to the observer's perspective, or does it exist universally?"

Lila Renn (Pragmatically Curious): Ah, what a fascinating topic! As someone who thrives on experimentation and innovation, I firmly believe that beauty is in the eye of the beholder. Different cultures, individuals, and experiences shape our perception of beauty.

賢者1-3 (中庸): But, my friends, isn't this just a fleeting notion? Shouldn't we strive for stability and harmony? Beauty, like truth, is absolute and can't be swayed by personal opinions or cultural differences.

Lila Renn: I understand your concerns, but don't you think that's too rigid? Beauty should be dynamic, ever-changing, just like our understanding of the world. It's not about finding a fixed answer; it's about embracing the uncertainty.

賢者1-3: But what about the wind, the symbol of change? Shouldn't we anchor ourselves to something solid?

Lila Renn: Ah, but that's precisely the point! The wind is precisely what makes life interesting. Without it, we'd stagnate. Beauty should be the result of embracing this uncertainty, not trying to control it.
