**絶対的イデオロギー:** Kaito Ryujin:  Traditionalism**

These two perspectives represent fundamentally different views on how to approach the concept of digital personhood. Individualism, represented by Akira Kazehaya and Rei Kurenai, emphasizes the importance of adapting to changing times and embracing new possibilities, such as digital personhood, which could lead to new forms of citizenship and participation in society.

On the other hand, Traditionalism, represented by Kaito Ryujin, prioritizes preserving traditional values, cultural heritage, and social norms, seeing digital personhood as a threat to these established institutions. This perspective emphasizes the importance of maintaining the status quo and protecting what has been passed down through generations.

These two ideologies are in direct opposition, with one emphasizing progress and innovation (Individualism) and the other emphasizing preservation and tradition (Traditionalism).
