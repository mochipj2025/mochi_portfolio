**絶対的イデオロギー:** 賢者3:  Objectivism**

These two perspectives represent the fundamental disagreements between 賢者2 (中庸) and 賢者3 (中庸) on one side, and Akira Kazehaya (進歩的Progressive) on the other.

Subjectivism argues that beauty is relative to the observer's perspective, and that it can be created or changed through human intervention. This view emphasizes the importance of connection with universal beauty, as 賢者3 suggests, in order to create new and dynamic forms of beauty.

Objectivism, represented by Akira Kazehaya, posits that there is an objective, absolute form of beauty that exists independently of human perception. According to this perspective, humans should strive to transcend past patterns and create new forms of beauty without being bound by traditional norms or natural laws.

These two opposing views reflect the complex dynamics of beauty, creativity, and cultural evolution.
