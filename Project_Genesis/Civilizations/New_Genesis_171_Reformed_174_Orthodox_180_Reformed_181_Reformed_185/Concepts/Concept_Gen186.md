# 新しい概念 (Shinsei Gaku)

## 定義

Shinsei Gaku refers to the creative process of giving a name or label to an abstract concept, such as the scent of a rainy day. This concept acknowledges that the act of naming can have a profound impact on our perception and understanding of reality.

## 意義

Shinsei Gaku is not just about assigning a name to a tangible object or phenomenon, but rather it's about recognizing the power of language in shaping our experiences and emotions. It suggests that by giving a name to something, we can tap into new possibilities, emotions, and connections. This concept encourages us to think creatively and find innovative ways to understand and interact with the world around us.

In the context of the discussion, Shinsei Gaku is about recognizing the potential for creative expression and innovation in naming the scent of a rainy day. It's not just about giving a name, but rather it's about acknowledging the power of language to shape our experiences and emotions. This concept can be applied to various aspects of life, from art and literature to science and technology.
