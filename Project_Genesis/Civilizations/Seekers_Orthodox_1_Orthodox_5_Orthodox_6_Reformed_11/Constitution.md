**絶対的イデオロギー:** 賢者1:  Non-Locality**

On one hand, "Determinism" (賢者1's view) emphasizes that time's irreversibility is a physical constraint, implying that our choices and actions are bound by the laws of physics. This perspective suggests that we should adapt to these constraints and live in accordance with them.

On the other hand, "Non-Locality" (賢者2's view) proposes that time's irreversibility is limited by our observational capabilities, and that there may be a higher-dimensional reality beyond our current understanding. This perspective suggests that we can penetrate deeper into the nature of time and space to uncover new possibilities.

These two ideas reflect fundamentally different understandings of time, free will, and the human condition.
