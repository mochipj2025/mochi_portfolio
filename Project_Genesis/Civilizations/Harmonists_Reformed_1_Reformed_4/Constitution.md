# Harmonists_Reformed_1_Reformed_4 憲法

**絶対的イデオロギー:** 賢者1:  Harmony**

In this debate, the three wise men present two opposing ideas. The first idea, Chaos, suggests that if words had physical weight, silence would be a sign of abundance. This thought is represented by Wise Man 3.

The second idea, Harmony, proposes that through communication, we can better understand the world and achieve harmony among individuals. This thought is represented by Wise Men 1 and 2.

The debate centers on the nature of language and its relationship to silence. The Chaotic perspective sees silence as a symbol of abundance, implying that words are burdensome and that silence allows for greater understanding. In contrast, the Harmonious perspective views silence as a means of empowering words, allowing us to better understand each other and achieve harmony.

The outcome of this debate is that Wise Man 1's Middle Ground approach is deemed the most excellent, with a social impact that increases power (+5) but decreases wealth (-10), leaving faith (0). This suggests that while language can be a source of strength, it is not without its costs.
