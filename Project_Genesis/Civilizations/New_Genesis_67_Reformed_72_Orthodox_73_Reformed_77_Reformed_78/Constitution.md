On one hand, Kaito Kuroba's perspective emphasizes individual freedom and economic freedom, prioritizing personal choices and self-determination. This is a classic example of individualism.

On the other hand, Lila Luna's argument focuses on the importance of community and social welfare, emphasizing the need for collective decision-making and shared values. This is a hallmark of communitarianism.

These two perspectives fundamentally disagree on the role of individual freedom versus communal well-being in determining societal value.
