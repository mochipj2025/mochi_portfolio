These two opposing views represent different philosophical approaches to understanding and addressing societal issues. Individualism emphasizes personal autonomy, flexibility, and adaptability, while Collectivism prioritizes social harmony, unity, and collective well-being.

In this context, Wise One 1 (Individualism) argues that people should be free to set their own sleep schedules based on individual needs and circumstances. In contrast, Wise One 2 (Collectivism) advocates for a standardized approach, arguing that uniform sleeping hours would promote social balance, prevent problems, and ensure medical care.

Wise One 3 (Balancing Individualism and Collectivism) suggests finding a middle ground by acknowledging both perspectives and striving for an equilibrium between individual needs and societal harmony.

This dichotomy reflects the ongoing debate in sociology about the relative importance of individual autonomy versus collective well-being, with significant implications for policy-making and social organization.
