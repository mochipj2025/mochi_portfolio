**絶対的イデオロギー:** Rei Rinrin:  Collectivism**

On one hand, we have Individualism, represented by Kaito Kiyomizu and Akira Arakawa, who emphasize the importance of freedom, equality, and personal autonomy. They argue that high levels of equality can stifle creativity and innovation.

On the other hand, we have Collectivism, represented by Rei Rinrin, who prioritizes balance, harmony, and coexistence between individuals and groups. They believe that true equality requires recognizing and valuing diversity and differences.

These two ideologies are fundamentally at odds, with Individualism promoting individual freedom and autonomy, while Collectivism emphasizes the importance of social cohesion and collective well-being.
