On the other hand, Idealism (賢者2) prioritizes living in the moment and valuing the present above all else. This perspective sees food as a fundamental right and a means to sustain life, rather than just a means to an end.

These two perspectives represent fundamentally different worldviews, with Realism emphasizing practicality and stability, while Idealism emphasizes passion and spontaneity.
