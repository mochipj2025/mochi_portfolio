Progressivism (Akira Shinsui's side) emphasizes the importance of preserving traditional values and cultural heritage while moving forward with new ideas and discoveries. This approach is cautious about abandoning established norms and seeks to balance innovation with stability.

Conservatism (Kaito Ryūjin's side), on the other hand, advocates for a more radical transformation, discarding traditional concepts like currency and creating a new, alternative society. This perspective prioritizes progress over preserving the status quo, embracing change as a means to reshape the future.

These two perspectives represent fundamentally different approaches to societal development, reflecting the ongoing debate between those who value tradition and those who seek innovation.
