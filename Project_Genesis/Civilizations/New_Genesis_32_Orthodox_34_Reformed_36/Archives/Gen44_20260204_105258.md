**属性**: social / CASUAL

**[Scene: The Great Plaza of New Genesis 32 Orthodox 34 Reformed 36]**

[The crowd murmurs as Kaito Kurenai, Akira Zenki, Renn Fujimoto, and the anonymous SAGE (賢者2) gather at the central stage.]

Kaito Kurenai: **"I think it's absurd to claim that perfect fairness hinders societal growth. What we need is a pragmatic approach that balances individual freedoms with collective well-being."**

Akira Zenki: **"But, dear friends, isn't the pursuit of perfection what drives us toward transcendence? Imperfection allows for innovation and self-discovery."**

Renn Fujimoto: **"In my opinion, this 'perfect fairness' is just a myth. People are naturally biased; we should focus on making the most of our time instead of worrying about ideal scenarios."**

賢者2 (anonymous SAGE): **"Perhaps we're overcomplicating things. What's essential is finding balance and harmony within ourselves, then radiating that balance into the world."**

Kaito Kurenai: **"I see your point, Zenki-san. But don't you think that striving for perfection can lead to stagnation? We need to find a middle ground between idealism and pragmatism."**

Akira Zenki: **"Ah, but what's the harm in aiming high, even if we don't quite reach it? The journey itself is where growth occurs."**

Renn Fujimoto: **"I agree with Kaito-san. Let's focus on making the most of our current situation instead of getting bogged down in hypotheticals."**

賢者2 (anonymous SAGE): **"You're all right, but maybe we should consider that perfect fairness might not be attainable. Perhaps it's futile to strive for something unattainable."**

[The crowd murmurs in agreement as the discussion comes to a close.]
