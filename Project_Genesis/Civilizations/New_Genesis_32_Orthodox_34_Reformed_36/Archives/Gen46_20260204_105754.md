(Akira Zenki) Ah, Kaito-san, you're too caught up in the literal meaning of words. Words are like whispers from the universe, carrying the essence of our souls. If silence had a physical weight, it would be a symphony of secrets and mysteries waiting to be unraveled.

(Kaito Kurenai) Ah, Renn-san, you're missing the point. Language is not just about expressing our thoughts; it's about shaping our reality. If we can't even agree on what constitutes "silence," how can we expect to make progress?

(Akira Zenki) Exactly! The weight of silence would reveal the depth of our souls. It would be a testament to our ability to listen, to empathize, and to understand.

(Renn Fujimoto) That's all well and good, Akira-san, but I think you're just trying to justify your own love of poetry and philosophy. In the real world, we don't have time for this nonsense.

(Kaito Kurenai) Ah, Renn-san, you're too quick to dismiss the power of language. Silence is not just the absence of sound; it's a dimension that holds its own gravity.

(Akira Zenki) Amen, Kaito-san! The weight of silence would be the anchor that grounds us in our humanity.
