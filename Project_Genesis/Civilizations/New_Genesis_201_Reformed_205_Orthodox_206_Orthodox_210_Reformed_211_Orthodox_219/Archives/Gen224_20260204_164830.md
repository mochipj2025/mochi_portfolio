**[Scene: The Grand Hall of New Genesis 201 Reformed, 224th Generation]**

**[Eiko (Enigmatic and introspective)]**: Ah, the concept of an afterlife. A topic that has plagued our kind for centuries. (pauses) As a sage who seeks balance, I propose that we acknowledge the existence of an afterlife as a way to bring people together, rather than tear them apart.

**[賢者1 (中庸)]**: But Eiko, what about the consequences? If we embrace this idea, won't it lead to chaos and disorder? We must prioritize stability above all else. (firmly)

**[Eiko]**: Stability is important, I agree. However, by acknowledging the afterlife, we can create a sense of community that transcends our physical existence. It's not about chaos or disorder; it's about understanding our place in the grand scheme.

**[賢者2 (中庸)]**: But what if this "afterlife" is just a myth? A mere fantasy created to comfort the grieving? We mustn't be swayed by sentimental notions and ignore the facts. (pragmatically)

**[Eiko]**: My dear colleague, even if it's just a myth, doesn't that say something about our capacity for hope and imagination? And isn't that what makes us human?

**[賢者3 (中庸)]**: I think we're getting off track. The real issue is how this concept affects our daily lives. If we spend too much time worrying about an afterlife, we'll neglect the problems plaguing our world today. (pragmatically)

**[Eiko]**: Ah, but that's where you're wrong. By acknowledging the afterlife, we can gain a new perspective on our current struggles. It's not about abandoning our duties here; it's about finding meaning in both.
