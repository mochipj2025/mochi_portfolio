**New Concept: "Augmentative Displacement"**

Markdown format:

# Augmentative Displacement
=========================

Augmentative displacement refers to the phenomenon where Artificial Intelligence (AI) both augments human capabilities by handling mundane tasks and displaces human workers, leading to a surplus of workers with outdated skills. This concept captures the essence of the debate between Lyra Flynn's Augmentationist view and Kael Jaxx's Displacementist perspective on the impact of AI on human employment.

Key aspects:

* **Augmentative**: AI handles mundane tasks, freeing humans to focus on creative pursuits and higher-level thinking.
* **Displacement**: AI replaces human workers, leading to a surplus of workers with outdated skills.
* **Surplus**: The job market becomes saturated, making it difficult for displaced workers to find new employment opportunities.

This concept highlights the complex interplay between technological advancements and societal adaptation. As AI continues to evolve, understanding augmentative displacement is crucial for developing effective strategies to mitigate its impact on human employment and ensure a more equitable distribution of benefits.
