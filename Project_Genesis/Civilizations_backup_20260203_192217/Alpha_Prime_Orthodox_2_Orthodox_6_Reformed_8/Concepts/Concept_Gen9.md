**New Concept:**

**"Intrinsic Motivation Framework"**

The Intrinsic Motivation Framework (IMF) posits that the relationship between Universal Basic Income (UBI) and individual motivation is not a straightforward one. Instead, it suggests that UBI's impact on motivation depends on the intrinsic motivations of individuals, which are influenced by factors such as personal values, interests, and autonomy.

**Key Components:**

1. **Intrinsic Motivation**: The desire to engage in an activity for its own sake, rather than for external rewards or pressures.
2. **Autonomy**: The freedom to make choices and take control of one's life, which is essential for intrinsic motivation.
3. **Interest**: The level of engagement and enjoyment individuals derive from an activity, which can either enhance or undermine intrinsic motivation.
4. **Values**: The personal values that guide individual behavior, such as a desire for self-improvement, creativity, or social responsibility.

**Theoretical Underpinnings:**

The IMF draws inspiration from Self-Determination Theory (SDT) and the concept of "intrinsic motivation" introduced by Edward Deci. SDT posits that human behavior is driven by three innate psychological needs: autonomy, competence, and relatedness. The IMF extends this framework to explore how UBI might interact with these needs to influence individual motivation.

**Implications for Policy:**

The IMF suggests that the effectiveness of UBI in promoting intrinsic motivation depends on its design and implementation. For instance:

* A UBI that allows individuals to pursue their passions without financial constraints may enhance intrinsic motivation.
* A UBI that is conditional or tied to specific behaviors may undermine autonomy and intrinsic motivation.
* A UBI that provides a basic level of economic security while also promoting education, training, and personal development may foster intrinsic motivation.

**Future Research Directions:**

1. Investigate the relationship between UBI and intrinsic motivation in different contexts (e.g., education, employment, entrepreneurship).
2. Explore how UBI's design and implementation influence its impact on intrinsic motivation.
3. Develop a more nuanced understanding of the interplay between autonomy, interest, values, and UBI.

By recognizing the complexity of human motivation and the role of UBI in shaping individual behavior, the IMF offers a framework for policymakers to consider when designing and implementing Universal Basic Income policies that promote intrinsic motivation and well-being.
