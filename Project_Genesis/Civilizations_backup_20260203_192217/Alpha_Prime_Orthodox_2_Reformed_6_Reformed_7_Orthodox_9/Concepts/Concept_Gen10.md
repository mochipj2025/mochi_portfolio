**New Concept:** "Synthetic Empathy"

Definition: Synthetic Empathy refers to the artificial intelligence system's ability to simulate human-like emotional understanding and connection with patients, while still maintaining its objectivity and analytical capabilities.

Rationale:

* The debate highlights the importance of empathy in healthcare, with both Augmentationists and Displacementists acknowledging its significance.
* However, the panelists also emphasize the limitations of AI in fully replicating human empathy, suggesting that a synthetic approach may be necessary to bridge this gap.
* Synthetic Empathy could enable AI systems to provide personalized and compassionate care, while still leveraging their analytical strengths.

Properties:

* **Emotional Intelligence**: Synthetic Empathy allows AI systems to recognize and respond to patients' emotional states, fostering trust and rapport.
* **Objectivity**: By separating emotional understanding from decision-making, Synthetic Empathy prevents AI from being swayed by personal biases or emotions.
* **Analytical Capabilities**: Synthetic Empathy combines with AI's analytical strengths to provide data-driven insights for patient care.

Potential Applications:

* Personalized medicine: Synthetic Empathy enables AI systems to tailor treatment plans to individual patients' needs and emotional states.
* Mental health support: AI-powered synthetic empathy can offer emotional support and counseling services, complementing human therapists.
* Healthcare education: Synthetic Empathy can facilitate patient education and engagement by simulating human-like interactions.

By recognizing the importance of empathy in healthcare and developing a synthetic approach, we can harness the benefits of Artificial Intelligence while preserving its limitations.
