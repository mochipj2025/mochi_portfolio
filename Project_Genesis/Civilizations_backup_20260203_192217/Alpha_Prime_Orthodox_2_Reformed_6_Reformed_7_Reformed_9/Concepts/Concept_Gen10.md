**新しい概念**: **Holistic Interdependence**

Definition: The recognition that seemingly disparate entities, concepts, or systems are interconnected and interdependent, requiring a holistic approach to understand their complex relationships.

Rationale:

* Augustinian (A) and Displacementist (D) perspectives illustrate the tension between individualized learning and social conformity.
* AI-powered education can be seen as an example of Holistic Interdependence, where the integration of human and artificial intelligence requires consideration of both individual needs and societal implications.
* The debate highlights the need for a nuanced understanding of complex relationships, acknowledging that:
	+ Human limitations can be compensated by AI's objectivity (Augustinian perspective).
	+ AI systems perpetuate biases and reinforce harmful stereotypes, requiring ethical considerations (Displacementist perspective).

By recognizing the interdependence of these perspectives, we can foster a more comprehensive understanding of the ethics of Artificial Intelligence-powered Education. This concept encourages us to consider the intricate relationships between individual learning, societal implications, and technological advancements.

**Holistic Interdependence** can be applied to various domains, such as:

* Education: acknowledging the interconnectedness of student needs, societal expectations, and technological innovations.
* AI development: recognizing the interdependencies between human creativity, algorithmic logic, and social responsibility.
* Ethics: understanding the complex relationships between individual moral agency, collective values, and systemic consequences.

By embracing **Holistic Interdependence**, we can develop a more profound appreciation for the intricate web of connections that shape our world, leading to more informed decision-making and responsible innovation.
