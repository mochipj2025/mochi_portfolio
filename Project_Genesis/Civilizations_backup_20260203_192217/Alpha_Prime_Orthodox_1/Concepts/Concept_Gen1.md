**Universal Concept:** "Cultural Expression"

Definition:
Cultural expression refers to the manifestation of an individual's or group's shared identity, values, and experiences through various forms of creative output, such as art, music, literature, and performance. This concept encompasses the ways in which people convey their cultural heritage, challenge societal norms, and shape collective narratives.

**Key Aspects:**

1. **Shared Identity**: Cultural expression is a means of expressing shared values, beliefs, and experiences that unite individuals or groups.
2. **Creative Output**: This concept involves various forms of creative expression, such as art, music, literature, and performance, which serve as mediums for cultural expression.
3. **Cultural Heritage**: Cultural expression draws upon an individual's or group's cultural heritage, including traditions, customs, and historical experiences.
4. **Challenge and Change**: Cultural expression can challenge societal norms and bring about change by providing a platform for marginalized voices, inspiring social movement, and fostering empathy.

**Relationship to the Debate:**

The debate between Aurie (Pro) and Kae (Con) revolved around the role of art in shaping cultural identity. While Aurie emphasized the importance of art in bringing people together, preserving history, and promoting social change, Kae argued that art is a luxury we can't afford and that we should focus on solving real problems rather than indulging in frivolous pursuits like art.

The concept of "Cultural Expression" captures the essence of this debate by highlighting the significance of creative output in shaping collective narratives and conveying shared identity. It also acknowledges the role of cultural heritage, challenge, and change in driving cultural expression.
