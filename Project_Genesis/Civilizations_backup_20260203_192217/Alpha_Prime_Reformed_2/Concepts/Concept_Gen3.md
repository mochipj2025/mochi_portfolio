**New Concept: "Eudaimonic Evolution"**

Definition:
Eudaimonic evolution refers to the process of societal transformation that balances technological advancements with human well-being, ensuring a harmonious coexistence between augmentation and displacement.

Key Features:

* **Augmentation**: AI-assisted tools enhance human capabilities, freeing us from mundane tasks and allowing for increased productivity and efficiency.
* **Displacement**: AI replaces certain human jobs, leading to the need for re-skilling and social security nets.
* **Evolution**: The adaptation of societal structures, economic systems, and individual skills to accommodate the changing landscape.

Core Principles:

1. **Coexistence**: Balancing the benefits of augmentation with the challenges of displacement, ensuring a harmonious relationship between humans and AI.
2. **Human-Centeredness**: Prioritizing human well-being, creativity, and critical thinking in the development and application of AI technologies.
3. **Adaptability**: Encouraging continuous learning, re-skilling, and upskilling to prepare individuals for the evolving job market.
4. **Social Security**: Implementing robust social security nets, including education, training programs, and job placement services, to support those affected by displacement.

In conclusion, eudaimonic evolution is a concept that acknowledges the dual nature of AI's impact on human employment and strives to create a future where technological advancements are harnessed for the betterment of humanity.
