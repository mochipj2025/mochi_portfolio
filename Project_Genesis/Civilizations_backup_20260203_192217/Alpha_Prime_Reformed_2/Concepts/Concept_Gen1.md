**New Concept:**

Universal Basic Income (UBI) can be redefined as a **Socially-Engineered Economic Buffer** that aims to mitigate the effects of systemic inequality and economic instability by providing a minimum level of financial security for all individuals, thereby promoting social cohesion, economic mobility, and overall well-being.

This concept abstracts away from the specific pros and cons discussed in the debate, focusing instead on UBI's potential to serve as a stabilizing force in society. It acknowledges that UBI can be both a safety net and an enabler of creativity and innovation, while also recognizing the need for careful consideration of its implementation and potential drawbacks.

**Key Features:**

1. **Socially-Engineered**: UBI is designed to address social issues, such as poverty and inequality, rather than solely focusing on economic growth.
2. **Economic Buffer**: UBI serves as a stabilizing force in the economy, providing a floor of financial security for all individuals.
3. **Minimum Level of Financial Security**: UBI ensures that everyone has access to a basic level of resources necessary for survival and well-being.

**Implications:**

1. **Social Cohesion**: UBI can promote social cohesion by reducing economic stress and increasing feelings of security and stability.
2. **Economic Mobility**: UBI can enable individuals to pursue their passions and interests, leading to increased creativity, innovation, and economic mobility.
3. **Systemic Change**: UBI can contribute to systemic change by challenging the current economic system's emphasis on growth and profit over people and planet.

By reframing UBI as a Socially-Engineered Economic Buffer, we can move beyond the binary debate between advocates and skeptics, and instead focus on the potential for UBI to serve as a powerful tool for creating a more just and equitable society.
