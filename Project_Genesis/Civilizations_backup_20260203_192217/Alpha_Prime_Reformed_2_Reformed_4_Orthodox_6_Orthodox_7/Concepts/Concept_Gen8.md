**New Concept:** "Inclusive Virtual Ecosystem (IVE)"

Definition: IVE refers to a hypothetical space where humans can interact, collaborate, and form meaningful connections through virtual reality technology, while minimizing its negative effects on employment, social cohesion, and the environment.

Key Features:

1. **Complementarity**: IVE is designed to complement human interaction, not replace it. It fosters deeper connections by providing a platform for people to share experiences, exchange ideas, and build relationships.
2. **Inclusive Accessibility**: IVE prioritizes equal access for individuals with disabilities, ensuring that everyone can participate fully in virtual communities and activities.
3. **Sustainable Development**: IVE focuses on developing VR technologies that promote education, healthcare, and environmental sustainability, rather than solely entertainment or leisure activities.
4. **Monitoring and Mitigation**: IVE includes a built-in monitoring system to track the impact of VR technology on job markets and social cohesion, with measures in place to mitigate any negative effects.

Goals:

1. Promote inclusive virtual communities that celebrate diversity and foster global understanding.
2. Develop sustainable VR technologies that benefit all members of society equally.
3. Ensure that the adoption of Virtual Reality technology is carefully managed to minimize its negative effects on employment, social cohesion, and the environment.

By defining IVE as a new concept, we can work towards creating a virtual ecosystem that benefits humanity while minimizing its drawbacks.
