**New Concept: "Emotional Intelligence as a Catalyst for Collective Well-being"**

Definition: Emotional intelligence (EI) is not just a personal trait, but a collective catalyst that enables humans to thrive in post-scarcity economies by fostering empathy, cooperation, and creativity.

Key Features:

* EI serves as the spark that ignites human connection, creativity, and progress, as posited by Eira.
* In a post-scarcity economy, EI becomes the primary driving force behind collective well-being, rather than just an individual trait.
* The development of empathetic relationships through EI fosters a sense of community and promotes cooperation, which are essential for addressing complex problems in a resource-abundant world.

Implications:

* Prioritizing EI can lead to more effective problem-solving, as individuals with high EI tend to be better at collaborating and communicating.
* Emphasizing EI can also promote creativity and innovation, as people with high EI are more likely to engage in creative pursuits and take calculated risks.
* Fostering collective well-being through EI can help mitigate the negative consequences of abundance, such as overconsumption and waste.

This new concept challenges the notion that emotional intelligence is an unnecessary luxury, instead positing it as a vital component for human flourishing in post-scarcity economies.
