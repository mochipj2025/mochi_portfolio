# 第3世代 議事録

**Alpha_Prime_Reformed_1: 3rd Generation Debate Log**

**Theme:** "Should the Council Implement a Universal Basic Income (UBI) for All Citizens?"

**Debate Summary:**

The debate began with a passionate plea from **Proponent Maria**: "Implementing UBI is crucial to ensure that all citizens have a safety net, allowing them to take calculated risks and pursue their passions. It's a moral imperative to eradicate poverty and promote economic mobility!"

**Counter-Pointed by Opponent Jax**: "UBI is a recipe for disaster! Implementing such a program would put an unsustainable strain on our already-thin resources. We can't afford to prioritize luxuries like unconditional handouts when our citizens are struggling to make ends meet. It's time to focus on education, skills training, and real economic growth!"

**Thought A: Prioritize Sustainability**

* **Proponent Maria**: "UBI is a sustainable solution that would actually reduce costs in the long run by addressing poverty and improving overall well-being."
* **Counter-Pointed by Opponent Jax**: "We can't afford to gamble with our citizens' futures on untested programs. We must prioritize short-term sustainability and resource management."

**Thought B: Prioritize Progress**

* **Proponent Maria**: "UBI would be a game-changer for innovation, allowing people the freedom to pursue their passions and contribute to society in meaningful ways."
* **Counter-Pointed by Opponent Jax**: "Progress requires sacrifice. We must prioritize growth over short-term gains and allow our citizens the opportunity to thrive through education and hard work."

**Conclusion:**

The debate highlighted the competing values of sustainability and progress, with proponents of UBI emphasizing its potential to promote economic mobility and improve overall well-being, while opponents focused on the potential risks and challenges associated with implementing such a program. As the Council weighs their options, they must carefully consider the long-term implications of their decision.
