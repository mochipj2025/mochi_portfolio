**Concept:** **Moral Autonomy**

Moral autonomy refers to the capacity for moral decision-making that is both informed by human values and independent of external influences. This concept encompasses the ability to make choices that align with one's personal moral compass, while also being open to refinement and adaptation through experience and reflection.

**Key Features:**

* **Informed Decision-Making**: Moral autonomy involves making decisions based on a deep understanding of one's values and principles.
* **Independence**: It requires the capacity to think critically and make choices that are not solely driven by external factors, such as AI systems or societal pressures.
* **Adaptability**: Moral autonomy implies an openness to learning from experiences and adapting one's moral framework in response to new information and perspectives.

**Implications:**

Moral autonomy has far-reaching implications for human morality, as it enables individuals to navigate the complexities of decision-making in a rapidly changing world. It highlights the importance of balancing technological advancements with human values and principles, ensuring that AI systems are designed and used in ways that promote moral growth and development.
