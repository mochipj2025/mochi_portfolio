**新しい概念：Interconnected Resilience**

: A philosophical concept that combines the ideas of interdependence, sustainability, and resilience to describe the capacity of a system or civilization to adapt, innovate, and thrive in the face of uncertainty and complexity.

**Definition:** Interconnected Resilience refers to the ability of a system or civilization to maintain its integrity and functionality while navigating the complexities of interdependence with other systems or civilizations. It involves the development of adaptive capacities, such as cooperation, diplomacy, and technological innovation, that enable the system or civilization to respond effectively to changing circumstances and challenges.

**Key Characteristics:**

* **Interconnectedness:** The recognition that the well-being of one system or civilization is inextricably linked with the well-being of others.
* **Adaptability:** The ability to adjust and innovate in response to changing circumstances and challenges.
* **Sustainability:** The capacity to maintain long-term viability while minimizing harm to the environment and other systems or civilizations.
* **Resilience:** The ability to withstand and recover from shocks, disruptions, or crises.

**Implications:**

* Prioritizing interconnected resilience can lead to more effective cooperation and diplomacy between civilizations, fostering a stronger foundation for galactic expansion.
* Embracing interconnected resilience can help mitigate the risks associated with reckless resource extraction, environmental degradation, and conflict over territory and resources.
* Developing adaptive capacities through innovation and technological advancement can enable civilizations to respond effectively to changing circumstances and challenges.
