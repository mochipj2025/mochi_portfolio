**Universal Concept: "Eudaimonic Security"**

Markdown format:

* **Definition**: Eudaimonic security refers to a state where individuals have access to basic necessities and opportunities for growth, allowing them to pursue their passions and contribute to society in meaningful ways.
* **Core Principles**:
	+ Ensuring a safety net (UBI) to eradicate poverty and promote economic mobility
	+ Prioritizing sustainability and resource management to ensure long-term viability
	+ Fostering progress through innovation, education, and skills training
* **Interconnectedness**: Eudaimonic security is linked to the concept of well-being, as it acknowledges that individuals' basic needs must be met before they can pursue higher-level goals.
* **Tensions**: Eudaimonic security may conflict with short-term sustainability concerns, highlighting the need for a balanced approach that considers both long-term and immediate needs.

This universal concept captures the essence of the debate, emphasizing the importance of providing a foundation for individuals to thrive while also considering the challenges and risks associated with implementing such a program.
