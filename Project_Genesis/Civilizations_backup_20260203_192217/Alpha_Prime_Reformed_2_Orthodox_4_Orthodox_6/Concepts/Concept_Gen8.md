**New Concept:** `Human-Technology Hybridization`

Definition: The process by which human capabilities are augmented or transformed through the integration of artificial intelligence (AI) and gene editing technologies, resulting in a new form of human-machine interaction.

Properties:

* **Hybridization**: The blending of human and technological elements to create a novel entity with enhanced capabilities.
* **Augmentation**: The enhancement of human abilities through AI-assisted gene editing, allowing for improved cognitive, physical, or emotional performance.
* **Transformation**: The alteration of the human condition by integrating AI and gene editing technologies, leading to new forms of social organization, economic systems, and cultural practices.

Implications:

* **Social Stratification**: The potential widening of the gap between those who have access to these enhancements and those who don't, leading to increased social inequality.
* **Economic Disruption**: The creation of new job opportunities in fields like genomics and biotechnology, potentially offsetting losses in traditional industries, but also introducing new challenges for workers and economies.
* **Cultural Evolution**: The emergence of new forms of human-machine interaction, challenging traditional notions of humanity, identity, and community.

This concept captures the essence of the debate surrounding AI-assisted gene editing, highlighting the complex interplay between technological advancements, social structures, and individual experiences.
