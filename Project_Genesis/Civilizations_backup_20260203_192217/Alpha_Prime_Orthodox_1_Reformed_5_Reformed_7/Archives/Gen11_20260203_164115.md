# 第11世代 議事録

**Log Entry 11.1: "The Fate of Humanity's Future"**

**Debate Summary:**

Date: 225,612 G.E.
Location: Galactic Senate Chamber, Alpha Prime Orthodox 1 Reformed 5 Reformed 7
Attendees: Representatives from various planetary colonies and spacefaring nations

**Theme:** "Should Humanity Prioritize Terraforming of Mars or Colonizing Other Planets in the Solar System?"

**Thought A (Expansionist):**

Proponent Representative Lyra from Nova Haven argued that humanity's future should be expanded beyond Earth. She emphasized the importance of securing a secondary planet for human habitation, citing concerns about environmental degradation and potential asteroid impacts on our home planet.

* Supporting arguments: The current rate of resource consumption will soon deplete resources; Mars has an ideal atmosphere for terraforming; establishing a foothold in the solar system ensures humanity's survival.

**Thought B (Conservative):**

Opponent Representative Arin from Terra Nova countered that caution and prudence should guide our decisions. She warned against overextending ourselves, citing the risks of contamination, ecological disruption, and potential catastrophic consequences if something goes awry during terraforming or colonization efforts.

* Supporting arguments: We have enough resources to sustain humanity within the solar system; Mars is still largely an uncharted environment with unknown implications for human habitation; prioritizing local development and conservation ensures our long-term sustainability on Earth.

**Key Points of Debate:**

1. The expansionist faction stressed the need for a backup plan, citing concerns about environmental degradation and potential asteroid impacts.
2. The conservative group emphasized the importance of protecting and preserving the integrity of our home planet, warning against overextending ourselves in the pursuit of colonization.
3. A proposal to establish a permanent human settlement on Mars was met with skepticism from Thought B proponents, who argued that this would divert resources away from terraforming and conservation efforts.

**Resolution:**

The debate concluded without reaching a consensus, with both sides agreeing to continue discussions and gather more data before making a decision. A committee was formed to explore the feasibility of terraforming Mars and colonizing other planets in the solar system, with representatives from both Thought A and Thought B included. The committee will present its findings at the next galactic senate meeting.
