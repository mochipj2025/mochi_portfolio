**新しい概念："Rational Pragmatism"**

Rational Pragmatism refers to the ability to balance competing values, costs, and benefits when making decisions, taking into account both the potential gains and risks involved. It involves considering multiple perspectives, conducting thorough analysis, and weighing the consequences of different courses of action before arriving at a conclusion.

In the context of the hyperloop debate, Rational Pragmatism would encourage decision-makers to carefully evaluate the pros and cons of investing in such a system, including its potential economic benefits, environmental impact, and risks. This approach recognizes that no solution is perfect, but instead seeks to find a reasonable and practical compromise that aligns with the city's values and priorities.
