# 第11世代 議事録

**Alpha Prime Orthodox 1.5, Generation 11 Debate Log**

**Theme:** Should the city prioritize a universal basic income (UBI) for all citizens?

**Date:** Alpha Cycle 321.213.15.23

**Moderator:** Councilor Elara Vex

**Debaters:**

* **Affirmative:** Senator Kael Arcturus, Representative of District I
* **Negative:** Councilor Lyra Flynn, Representative of District III

**Opening Statements:**

Senator Kael Arcturus (Affirmative): "A universal basic income is not a handout, but a fundamental human right. It would provide a safety net for our most vulnerable citizens, allowing them to pursue their passions and contribute to the economy in meaningful ways."

Councilor Lyra Flynn (Negative): "UBI would be a financial burden on the city, diverting resources away from essential services like education and healthcare. It's not a sustainable solution to poverty or inequality."

**Rebuttals:**

Senator Kael Arcturus: "The current system perpetuates inequality by relying solely on market forces. UBI would address the root cause of poverty, empowering individuals to make choices that benefit themselves and society as a whole."

Councilor Lyra Flynn: "There's no empirical evidence UBI would achieve these lofty goals. In fact, it would create an artificial dependence on government support, stifling innovation and entrepreneurship."

**Cross-Examination:**

Senator Kael Arcturus: "How can you justify the status quo when our city's wealth gap continues to widen?"

Councilor Lyra Flynn: "We need to focus on education and job training programs to uplift marginalized communities. Handouts won't solve this issue; real-world skills will."

**Closing Statements:**

Senator Kael Arcturus: "UBI is a moral imperative, recognizing the inherent value of every human life. It's time for us to lead by example and prioritize compassion over profit."

Councilor Lyra Flynn: "We must prioritize practical solutions that address poverty and inequality. UBI is an untested experiment; let's focus on tried-and-true methods to build a more just society."

**Voting Results:**

Affirmative: 5 votes (Senator Kael Arcturus, Representative of District I)
Negative: 6 votes (Councilor Lyra Flynn, Representative of District III)

**Result:** The motion "Should the city prioritize a universal basic income for all citizens?" is rejected.
