# 第12世代 議事録

**Debate Log 12th Generation**

**Theme:** "Should Alpha Prime Orthodox 1.5 Invest in Advanced Biometric Security Measures?"

**Moderator:** Aurora W.

**Affirmative Team (In Favor):**

* Elara V.: "By implementing advanced biometric security measures, we can ensure the safety and security of our citizens' personal data and prevent potential threats to our city's infrastructure."
* Kael T.: "Biometric technology is already being used in various industries; it's only logical that we adopt it to safeguard our city. It's a necessary step towards building a more secure future."

**Negative Team (Against):**

* Zara X.: "We can't just blindly follow the trend and invest in biometric security measures without considering the potential risks and implications on individual privacy. We need to think critically about the consequences of such technology."
* Renn S.: "What's the guarantee that these measures won't be exploited by malicious actors? We should focus on more effective, tried-and-true methods for ensuring our city's safety."

**Key Points:**

Affirmative:

* Emphasized the importance of prioritizing security in an increasingly digital age
* Highlighted the benefits of biometric technology in preventing identity theft and securing sensitive information

Negative:

* Raised concerns about potential privacy violations and the lack of transparency in biometric data collection
* Suggested that alternative, more traditional methods could be just as effective in ensuring city safety

**Notable Quotes:**

* "The devil is in the details. We need to make sure we're not sacrificing our citizens' right to privacy for the sake of security." - Zara X.
* "It's about finding a balance between security and freedom. Biometric technology can be a valuable tool, but it's not a magic solution." - Renn S.

**Outcome:** The debate concluded with no clear winner, as both sides presented valid points. The council will consider the arguments and consult with experts before making a decision on whether to invest in advanced biometric security measures.
