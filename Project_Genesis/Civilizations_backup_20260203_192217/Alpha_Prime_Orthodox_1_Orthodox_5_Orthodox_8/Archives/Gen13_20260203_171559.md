"Implementing a mandatory recycling program is crucial for the sustainability of our city," said Mayor Blackwood. "It's a simple yet effective way to reduce waste and conserve resources."

"Education is important, but it's not enough," countered Dr. Kaelin. "With a mandatory program, we can ensure that every resident participates in recycling, regardless of their level of knowledge or commitment."

Negation:

"A one-size-fits-all approach will only lead to frustration and non-compliance," said Mr. LaSalle. "We need to prioritize individual freedom and choice when it comes to waste management."

**Counter-Rebuttals:**

Affirmative:

"Individual freedom is important, but so is the greater good," replied Mayor Blackwood. "As a city, we have a responsibility to protect our environment and ensure a sustainable future for generations to come."

Negation:

"The greater good doesn't justify imposing unnecessary regulations on our citizens," argued Councilor Patel. "We should focus on creating a culture of sustainability through education and community engagement instead."

**Conclusion:**

The debate ended with no clear consensus, leaving the issue of mandatory recycling at a stalemate. The Alpha Prime Orthodox 1.5 City Council will need to deliberate further to reach a decision.
