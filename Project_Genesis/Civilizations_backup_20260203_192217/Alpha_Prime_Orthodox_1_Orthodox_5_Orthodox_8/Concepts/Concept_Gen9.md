**Universal Concept: "Technological Mediation of Human Experience"**

### Definition

Technological mediation of human experience refers to the process by which technology intervenes between humans and their surroundings, influencing the way they perceive, interact with, and make sense of the world.

### Characteristics

* **Intermediation**: Technology acts as an intermediary between humans and their environment, altering the ways in which people experience reality.
* **Mediation of Perception**: Technology shapes how we perceive and understand the world, influencing our cognition and emotions.
* **Augmentation of Human Capabilities**: Technology enhances or extends human abilities, allowing us to interact with the world in new and innovative ways.
* **Risk of Dependence**: The increased reliance on technology raises concerns about the potential negative consequences of over-reliance, such as decreased critical thinking skills.

### Implications

The concept of technological mediation has significant implications for various aspects of human life, including education, communication, entertainment, and personal relationships. It is essential to carefully consider these implications when deciding whether to adopt new technologies or integrate them into our daily lives.
