# 第6世代 議事録

**話題**: 通貨という概念を排除した文明における価値の定義
**属性**: social / CASUAL
**白熱度**: 🔥🔥 (49)

---

**Seekers' Record: 6th Generation Discussion**

**Date:** 2154.02.15

**Location:** Community Hub, Sector 3

** Participants:** Kaito, Lila, Taro, Mika, and Hiro

**Moderator:** None appointed (informal discussion)

**Recorded by:** Seekers' Chronicler

Kaito: Hey guys, have you ever thought about what we value in this society without money?

Lila: Hmm... I think it's all about relationships. We prioritize people over things.

Taro: Yeah, that makes sense. Without currency, we focus on shared experiences and mutual support.

Mika: But how do we quantify those values? Can we put a price tag on them?

Hiro: Actually, I think that's the beauty of it. We don't need to reduce everything to numbers or commodities. Our worth is derived from our connections with each other.

Kaito: Exactly! And that's what makes this society so unique. We're not driven by material possessions or financial gain.

Lila: It's like we've transcended the idea of wealth and poverty. Everyone has equal value in this sense.

Taro: Absolutely. And it's amazing to see how our community comes together when someone needs help. That's what I mean by shared experiences.

Mika: But don't you think that'll lead to complacency? I mean, if everyone's doing well, won't we become too comfortable?

Hiro: Ah, that's where the Seekers come in, right? We're always pushing for innovation and growth. There are still challenges to overcome, but our values align with those goals.

Kaito: That's true. And I think it's essential to recognize that our values evolve along with our understanding of the world. So even without currency, we'll continue to find new ways to define value and make progress.

(Conversation continues, flowing naturally around the topic.)

**End of Record**

This discussion highlights the Seekers' communal focus on relationships, shared experiences, and mutual support as the foundation of their values system. The absence of a traditional monetary system encourages a shift toward valuing connections over material possessions, leading to a more equitable society where everyone's worth is recognized.
