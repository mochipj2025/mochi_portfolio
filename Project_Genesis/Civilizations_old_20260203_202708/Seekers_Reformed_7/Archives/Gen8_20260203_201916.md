**Opening Remarks**

Kaida: Good evening, everyone. Tonight, we'll be discussing the theme "Primitive Sense of Comfort in Waiting in Line." As you're all aware, our community values efficiency and productivity. But have you ever stopped to think about what lies beneath our willingness to wait in line?

**Initial Statements**

Renn (Affirmative): I believe that waiting in line taps into a deep-seated sense of comfort. You see, when we're among others doing the same thing, it creates a sense of community and belonging. We feel less lonely, less anxious.

Lirien (Negative): But isn't this just a facade? A way to avoid the discomfort of truly being alone with our thoughts? I think waiting in line is more about escapism than genuine comfort.

Arin (Affirmative): I disagree. When we're waiting in line, we're forced to confront our own mortality. It's a reminder that we're all equal, regardless of our achievements or status. This shared experience creates a sense of solidarity.

Valtor (Negative): But what about the power dynamics at play? Those who are wealthy and influential often get priority treatment, leaving the rest of us waiting. Is this really a symbol of comfort?

**Heated Debate**

Renn: You're all missing the point! Waiting in line is about shared experience, about being part of something bigger than ourselves.

Lirien: That's naive! It's about social hierarchy and control!

Arin: But think about it – waiting in line is one of the few times we can truly let our guard down. We're not trying to impress anyone; we're just existing.

Valtor: Existing? You call that comfort? I'd rather take my chances elsewhere, thank you very much!

**Emotional Tension**

Kaida: Okay, let's take a step back. It seems like we've reached an impasse. Can we find common ground?

Renn: I think we need to acknowledge that waiting in line is complex. It can be both comforting and uncomfortable depending on the context.

Lirien: Agreed. But we must also recognize the power dynamics at play.

Arin: And acknowledge the shared experience, no matter how fleeting.

Valtor: Fine. But let's not sugarcoat it – waiting in line is a necessary evil.

**Conclusion**

Kaida: Thank you all for your thoughtful contributions to this discussion. It seems that while we may not have resolved our differences, we've at least acknowledged the complexity of the issue. Perhaps, as Seekers Reformed 7, we can strive to create systems that allow us to balance efficiency with shared experience and a sense of community.
