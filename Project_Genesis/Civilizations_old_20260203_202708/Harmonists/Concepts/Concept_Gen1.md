# 概念名：Simulationality

## 定義：Simulationality refers to the idea that reality is a simulation or a creation of a higher power or advanced civilization. It suggests that our experiences and actions may not be entirely real, but rather part of a larger program or design.

## 意義：Simulationality challenges our understanding of free will and the nature of reality. If we are living in a simulated universe, then our choices and decisions may not have the impact we think they do. It also raises questions about the purpose and motivations of the simulator(s), if there is one. Simulationality encourages us to consider the possibility that our existence is part of a larger narrative or design, which can lead to new perspectives on our place in the universe and our responsibilities within it.
