**絶対的イデオロギー:** 正統派: From the conversation, I extracted two opposing ideas:

**Individualism vs Collectivism**

松浦一郎 (Matsurou Ichirou) and 小林光太郎 (Kobayashi Kōtarou) seem to lean towards collectivism, emphasizing the idea that people are influenced by others' actions and share a sense of comfort in doing so. They view this phenomenon as a natural instinct, comparing it to ancient communal living or even children's playtime.

On the other hand, 高橋恵子 (Takahashi Keiko) appears to represent individualism, highlighting the uncertainty and unpredictability that lies beneath the surface of social conformity. She notes that people may not necessarily think about what others are thinking, but still move forward in a crowd.

These two perspectives seem to be in tension with each other:

* Collectivism emphasizes the importance of group dynamics and shared experiences.
* Individualism highlights the potential for personal thoughts, desires, and motivations to drive human behavior.
