[0:00 - 5:00]

Moderator: Good evening, everyone. Tonight's topic is the concept of afterlife and its impact on society. Pastor Elara and Dr. Kaito will present their perspectives as proponents of this concept.

Pastor Elara: Thank you, Sophia. As a theologian, I believe that the idea of an afterlife provides hope and comfort to those who have lost loved ones. It gives us a sense of continuity and connection beyond physical life.

Dr. Kaito: That's right. The concept of afterlife also has implications for our understanding of human psychology and personal growth. It suggests that we are more than just biological entities, but have a spiritual essence that transcends this life.

[5:00 - 10:00]

Engineer Renn: (interrupting) Excuse me, can I ask a question?

Pastor Elara: Of course.

Engineer Renn: How do you justify the existence of an afterlife without empirical evidence? It seems like a convenient way to explain away the mystery of death.

Dr. Kaito: Well, we have anecdotal accounts of near-death experiences and reports of people experiencing visions or hearing voices after their physical passing...

Engineer Renn: (skeptical) That's not the same as empirical evidence! You're just grasping at straws to sustain your beliefs.

[10:00 - 20:00]

Biologist Emiko: (interrupting) I don't think we should dismiss the concept of afterlife entirely. Even if it's not empirically proven, it can still have a profound impact on people's emotional well-being and moral compass. It's like the placebo effect in medicine – even if it doesn't work, people find comfort knowing that something is working.

Pastor Elara: Exactly! The concept of afterlife provides a framework for understanding and coping with mortality.

Engineer Renn: (outraged) You're saying that just because people believe something will make them feel better, we should tolerate religious dogma?!

Biologist Emiko: (calmly) No, I'm saying that while the scientific community may not be able to prove or disprove the concept of afterlife, it's not our place to dictate what people find comforting. Humanism emphasizes individual agency and emotional resilience...

[20:00 - 30:00]

Dr. Kaito: (passionately) This is where psychology comes in! Our experiences, emotions, and values are all rooted in our understanding of the present moment. If we don't have hope for a better future or a connection to something greater than ourselves...

Pastor Elara: (interrupting) That's exactly what I'm saying! The concept of afterlife gives us that sense of transcendence!

Engineer Renn: (exasperated) You're both ignoring the practical implications! This is not just about personal comfort; it has real-world consequences. People are living in fear of hellfire or eternal damnation...

Biologist Emiko: (calmly) I think we can disagree without being disagreeable. Let's focus on creating a society that values compassion, empathy, and understanding.
