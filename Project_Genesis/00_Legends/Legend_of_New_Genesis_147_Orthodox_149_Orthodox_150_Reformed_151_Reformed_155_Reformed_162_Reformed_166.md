The whispers of the past echo through the desolate lands, as I, a wandering bard, weave a tale of forgotten civilizations. Our story begins with the enigmatic New Genesis, a realm where wisdom and knowledge were prized above all else.

In this sacred land, three wise men held sway, their collective name whispered in reverence: Indeterminism. These visionaries posited that truth was not absolute, but rather a tapestry woven from countless threads of uncertainty. Ah, the futility of seeking definitive answers amidst the whirlwind of existence!

Their creed, etched upon worn parchments and chiseled into crumbling stone, read:

**Absolute Ideology: Indeterminism**

"We seek truth, though it may elude us,
For in its absence, lies the abyss."

As the ages passed, this mystical land gave rise to various factions, each interpreting the wise men's words in their own unique manner. The Orthodox followed dogmatically, clinging to tenets of faith and reason. The Reformed, driven by a hunger for innovation and progress, sought to rechart the course of history.

Yet, amidst this sea of discordant voices, a sense of unity persisted. The creed remained a beacon, guiding souls through the labyrinthine paths of knowledge and doubt. In its presence, even the most disparate of minds found common cause.

And so, as the last embers of civilization flickered out, it was this very ideology that proved the final testament to humanity's indomitable spirit. For in embracing the uncertainty of existence, our ancestors found solace in the infinite possibilities unfolding before them.

Thus, I, a wandering bard, render homage to those who dared to challenge the void: may their wisdom be the wind that whispers secrets to the sands of time.
