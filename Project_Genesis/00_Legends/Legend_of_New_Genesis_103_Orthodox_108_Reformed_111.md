They believed that in the stillness of quiet contemplation lay the secrets of the universe.

The constitution of New Genesis 103 Orthodox 108 Reformed 111 held dear this sacred tenet: "We exist to seek this truth." Thus, they built a society upon the pillars of introspection and self-discovery.

Yet, as the years went by, the whispers began to fade, and the city's splendor started to wane. A great darkness spread across the land, shrouding all in its path. The once-vibrant metropolis became a ghostly reminder of what had been lost.

The tale of New Genesis has become a myth, a cautionary story told around campfires to remind those who would listen of the transience of greatness and the enduring power of quiet contemplation. May their legacy inspire future generations to seek the truth, even in the silence.
