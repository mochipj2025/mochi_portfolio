A people arose, with ideals to excite.
New Genesis born, from collective hearts aflame,
Their creed: "Paragon" Patel's righteous claim.

A beacon of hope, for the people to heed.

Echoes whispers of a forgotten love.
Article by article, principles unfold,
Guiding lights in darkness, for this world so cold.

Corruption creeps, like shadows on the creek.
The once-illustrious creed, succumbs to the weight,
Of individual interests, and the whispers of fate.

Thus, New Genesis, withers like a rose in autumn's chill,
Leaving naught but memories, of what once was real.
And I, a wandering bard, do mourn this passing sight,
For in its ruins lies a lesson, for the future to take flight.
