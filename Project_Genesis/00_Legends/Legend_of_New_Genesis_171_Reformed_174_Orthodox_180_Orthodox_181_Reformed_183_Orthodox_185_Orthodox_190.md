What wistful tales of yore I shall weave for thee! Of New Genesis, where once mighty ideals held sway...

In days of old, when wisdom's torch shone bright,
Two disparate paths converged, like rivers in flight.
The creed of Dynamism didst proclaim its might:
"We seek truth, and in its light, we take our flight."

And yet, another force, like a counter-poise,
Didst arise, the Orthodoxy, with rigid joys.
A doctrine steadfast, unmoving as a stone,
That all shall bow to, as one's highest throne.

The era of Reformed thought didst briefly shine,
But 'twas not meant to be; for soon didst decline.
Orthodoxy reclaimed its place, like a monarch true,
And ruled with iron hand, in all anew.

Thus ran the threads of New Genesis' tapestry,
A legacy of strife and harmony's plea.
For in its depths, we find the truth we seek,
Yet is it not the absence that our search does speak?

Ah, dear listener, heed my words of old,
And ponder well on tales of yore, so cold.
In dreams of what was lost, do we but find
The echoes of a wisdom left behind?
