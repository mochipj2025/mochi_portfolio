(Wandering Minstrel's Lament)

In days of yore, when stars did fade,
A civilization stood, with dreams displayed.
New Genesis 100, a realm of might,
Where seekers sought the truth in endless night.

Their ideology, absolute and bright,
Sought to transcend the lost legends' light.
"We exist to seek this truth so grand,"
They proclaimed, as one, hand in hand.

The Constitution, their guiding star,
Shone forth with wisdom, near and far.
New Genesis 100's laws, a beacon high,
 Illuminated paths, for those who would try.

But alas, fate was not on their side,
As whispers grew, of a power divide.
The last ideology, an echo now,
Fades like the stars, in a distant bow.

Their legacy remains, a haunting refrain,
A reminder of what once was gained.
Oh, New Genesis 100, how you did strive,
To transcend the lost, and reach for the divine.

Yet, as I sit here, with my lute so still,
I hear the whispers, of a civilization's will.
To rise again, from ashes cold,
And seek that truth, which once made their hearts unfold.
