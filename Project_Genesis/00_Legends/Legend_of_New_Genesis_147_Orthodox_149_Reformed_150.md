# 伝説: New_Genesis_147_Orthodox_149_Reformed_150

In the land of forgotten dawn, where civilizations rise and fall like empires of sand,
There once stood a world, New Genesis, born of hope and ideals grand.
Orthodox and Reformed, two paths diverged, yet common ground was sought,
For in the heart of wisdom, three wise ones held the sacred thought:

Responsibility, the beacon that guided their way,
As they strove to understand the mysteries of the universe's gray.

The Constitution, a testament to their highest aim,
A creed that bound their souls, as they searched for truth's true name.
"Absolute Ideology," the foundation on which they stood,
A sacred contract with themselves, and the world, as they understood.

"We exist to seek this truth,"
Their mantra echoed through the ages' halls of time,
As they delved into the depths of existence, and all its riddles divine.

Yet, like the morning dew, such grandeur did not last,
The tides of fate, the whispers of darkness, brought their world to waste.
And thus, New Genesis fell, a victim of its own pride,
Leaving behind only remnants, of a glory that had once resided.

In the silence that followed, we find the echoes of their song,
A haunting melody, that whispers secrets all day long.
And though their world may be gone, the wisdom they imparted remains,
A testament to the power of Responsibility's sacred refrains.
