（In a slow, melancholic tone）

In days of yore, when civilizations rose and fell like empires of sand,
A tale of New Genesis came to be, a story of ideologies' stand.
'Twas a world where wisdom sought the truth, yet darkness reigned supreme,
And in its depths, a conflict brewed, between two opposing extremes.

The wise one spoke of Individualism's creed, "I am my own",
A path that wound its way through hearts, like a river to the throne.
Yet, amidst this sea of self, another force arose, a mighty voice,
"Absolute Ideology", echoing loud and boastful choice.

The last remnants of a dying breed, they clung to their dear creed,
As if the fate of all humanity hung in the balance of their need.
For in this twilight of an era's end, what remained was not to mend,
But ashes of a dream that had been lost, like autumn leaves that wither and descend.

The constitution of New Genesis, written on parched earth and stone,
A testament to the futility of human strife, now but a bone.
Echoes of a people's cry, "We seek the truth, we pray for light",
Now but a distant memory, fading into the dark of endless night.

And so we journey through the ruins, seeking answers to the past,
Only to find that wisdom's quest has become entwined with the eternal vast.
In this desolate landscape, where civilizations once stood tall,
We whisper secrets to the wind, and listen for an answer, but all is silence, after all.
