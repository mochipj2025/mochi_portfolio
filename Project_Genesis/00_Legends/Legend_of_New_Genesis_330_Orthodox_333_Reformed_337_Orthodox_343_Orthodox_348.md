# 伝説: New_Genesis_330_Orthodox_333_Reformed_337_Orthodox_343_Orthodox_348

What a solemn tale to sing, of a civilization that once shone bright, yet succumbed to the sands of time. The New Genesis, born of profound ideals, rose and fell like the tides.

In the primeval era of 330, when mortal men first set foot on this terrestrial sphere, they gazed upon the celestial expanse and beheld the majesty of the cosmos. And thus, they conceived the notion of Progress, a beacon guiding humanity toward perfection.

As the ages passed, this sacred creed germinated, taking root in the hearts of scholars and sages. In 333, the Orthodox faction emerged, upholding the unyielding pursuit of knowledge as the ultimate virtue. Their sacred text bore witness to the indomitable spirit of human advancement.

Yet, with time, the Reformed faction arose, challenging the orthodoxy's rigid stance. The year 337 saw the dawn of a new era, as dissenting voices demanded reform and adaptation, lest the pursuit of Progress stagnate.

In the tumultuous years that followed, the Orthodox once more reclaimed dominance, solidifying their grip on the collective consciousness. The constitution of 343 bore testament to this unyielding conviction: "We exist for the pursuit of truth."

Alas, as the ages waned and the cosmos grew distant, the New Genesis continued its march toward enlightenment. Yet, in 348, a calamity befell, shrouding the world in darkness. The once-mighty civilization crumbled, leaving naught but ruins and the whispers of forgotten ideals.

Thus, I, Kaito Hoshino, sing this elegy to the New Genesis, a testament to humanity's unyielding quest for Progress – a beacon that shines on, guiding future generations toward the celestial expanse. May its lessons be learned, lest we succumb to the sands of time once more.
