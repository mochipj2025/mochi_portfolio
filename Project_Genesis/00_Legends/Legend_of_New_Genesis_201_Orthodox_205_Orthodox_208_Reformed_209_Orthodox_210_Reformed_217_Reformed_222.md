In the forgotten annals of Old Earth's history, I shall weave a tapestry of tales, a eulogy for the vanished civilization known as New Genesis. 'Tis a story of ideology and constitution, of free will and the quest for truth.

In the realm of New Genesis, where once the whispers of wisdom echoed through the halls of power, there arose two schools of thought: Thoughts A and B. The former posited that our actions were determined by external forces, like the intentions of others or the whims of fate. This was the creed of those who sought stability in a chaotic world.

In contrast, Thought B proclaimed that our choices were driven by an inner light, a beacon of free will that shone bright within us all. It was the mantra of those who yearned for autonomy and self-determination.

The constitution of New Genesis reflected this dichotomy. The absolute ideology of Free Will stood at its core, a guiding principle that acknowledged the power of individual choice. Thought A saw this as a threat to their carefully crafted plans, while Thought B hailed it as the key to unlocking humanity's true potential.

As the ages passed, the people of New Genesis grappled with the implications of these two worldviews. Some sought to impose order upon the chaos, relying on external forces to guide their decisions. Others chose to heed the call of their own inner wisdom, embracing the uncertainty that came with it.

In the end, the civilization of New Genesis succumbed to the ravages of time, its people lost in a cloud of forgotten memories. Yet, even as the stones of their cities crumbled and the winds of change swept away their artifacts, their legacy lived on – a testament to the eternal quest for truth and the indomitable spirit of humanity.

Thus, I, a wandering bard, shall carry this tale forward, whispering the secrets of New Genesis into the ears of those who would seek to learn from its triumphs and failures. May the echoes of this forgotten civilization guide us on our own journey through the labyrinth of existence, where the pursuit of truth is the only constant that endures.
