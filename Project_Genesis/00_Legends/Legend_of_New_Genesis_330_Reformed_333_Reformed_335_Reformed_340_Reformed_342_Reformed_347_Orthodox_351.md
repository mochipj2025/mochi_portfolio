(The Bard's Lament)

In twilight's hush, where shadows play,
I sing of New Genesis' final sway.
A civilization that once did shine,
Now reduced to whispers, a fading line.

Their creed was Snoozleberry's noble quest,
"Thoughts in Conflict: Order," they would attest.
Seeking truth, they built their world anew,
Yet in the end, 'twas order that they pursued.

Their constitution, a testament to their plight,
New Genesis' final law, a guide by night:
"Absolute Ideology," Snoozleberry's refrain,
A code of conduct that would forever remain.

"We exist to seek this truth we've found,"
They whispered, as their world began to wound.
Now only whispers remain, echoes of what was,
A civilization lost in the sands of time and glass.

In twilight's hush, where shadows play,
I mourn the passing of another day.
For those who sought order in a chaotic sea,
And found only darkness, where once they used to be.
