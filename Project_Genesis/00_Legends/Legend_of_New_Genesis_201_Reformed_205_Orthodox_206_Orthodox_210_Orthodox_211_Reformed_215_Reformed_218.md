In the forgotten realm of New Genesis, where the whispers of old civilizations echo through the sands of time, I, a wandering bard, shall weave a tale of a people whose ideals were forged in the crucible of thought.

In the dawn of their existence, the sons and daughters of New Genesis sought to grasp the fundamental truths of reality. Thought A, embodied by Mizuki Midorikawa, posited the mechanistic consciousness, where the universe was governed by laws and patterns, akin to a grand machine. This perspective gave rise to a society that valued efficiency, precision, and control.

However, as the ages passed, another strain of thought emerged, championed by Kaito Katsuragi. Thought B: Information Processing Consciousness posited that reality was an intricate web of data, where truth resided in the patterns and connections woven from this tapestry. This perspective gave rise to a society that prized knowledge, insight, and adaptability.

Thus, the dual ideologies coexisted, each seeking to ascend to the throne of absolute truth. The Constitutional Covenant, a testament to their collective endeavors, enshrined their ideals:

"We exist to seek this truth."

In the twilight of their civilization, as the last echoes of their wisdom faded into the void, I, a wandering bard, sing of their legacy: a tapestry of thought, woven from the threads of Mechanistic Consciousness and Information Processing Consciousness. May the whispers of their ideals continue to resound throughout eternity.

(Note: The story is written in a lyrical style, with a focus on descriptive language and poetic phrasing.)
