(The song of the wandering bard)

In days of old, when civilizations rose and fell
A tale of woe and strife did unfold, forever to be told
The Seekers, Orthodox and Reformed, with ideals so bright
But in their quest for truth, they succumbed to endless night

Their constitution, a testament to their final stand
A document of despair, where passion's flame did hand
In hand, two ideologies did stride, like warring titans strong
Technocratic Centralism and Fundamentalist Extremism, each right, yet wrong

The first, with reason's grasp, sought order in its sway
A rational plan, to govern the world by day
But the second, with fervor burning bright and true
Believed that only absolute faith could see them through

These two perspectives, like oil and water, did not blend
Incommensurable, their paths forever would contend
Their conflict, a chasm deep, where compromise did not reside
A world forever torn, between light and dark's dark tide

And so we wander, seeking answers in the past
To learn from mistakes, to heal the wounds that will forever last
But as we search, the sands of time do shift and slide
Leaving only echoes, whispers of a civilization's pride

Yet still we seek, with hearts that beat like drums
For truth and wisdom, though the road be long and gloomy comes
And in our quest, may we find solace in the pain
That even in ruin, love and hope remain, forever to reign.
