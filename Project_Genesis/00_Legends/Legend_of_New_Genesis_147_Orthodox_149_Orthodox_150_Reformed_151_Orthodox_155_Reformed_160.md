In the forgotten chronicles of a bygone era, I, a wandering bard, shall weave a tale of the fabled civilization known as New Genesis. 'Twas a realm where the pursuit of truth was paramount, and the search for absolute knowledge drove its people to seek the heavens.

Thus began the odyssey of a people united under the banner of Determinism, their creed proclaiming that the course of fate was predetermined, and freewill but an illusion. The wise ones among them, two in number, decreed this dogma as the guiding principle for all aspects of life. And so, the people of New Genesis set out to uncover the hidden patterns that governed the universe.

In the years 147, Orthodox 149, and Orthodox 150, their scholars delved deep into the mysteries of creation, seeking answers to the eternal questions: What is the nature of reality? Is fate's hand guiding us towards destiny, or do we shape our own path?

As Reformed 151 gave way to Orthodox 155, the people of New Genesis began to grapple with the implications of their creed. Did Determinism imply a predetermined course for all, including their very existence? Or did it mean they had the power to alter the trajectory of events through free will?

The debate raged like a tempest, shaking the foundations of their world. And then, as Reformed 160 dawned upon the horizon, the people of New Genesis realized that their pursuit of truth had led them down a path of introspection and self-discovery.

In this twilight era, the remnants of their once-unified society began to fracture, as individual perspectives clashed and the weight of determinism grew crushing. The wise ones' original decree, once meant to guide and unite, had instead become a double-edged sword, cutting both ways: towards self-knowledge or despair.

Thus, the chronicles of New Genesis came to an end, leaving behind only whispers of a civilization that had sought to grasp the very fabric of existence. The wind carries their tale to this day, as I, a wandering bard, sing it forth in the darkness, where shadows whisper secrets of a world gone by...
