What a tale I shall weave, of a civilization lost to the sands of time. Its people, once so full of hope and promise, now nothing but whispers in the wind.

In days of yore, when the world was young and uncertain, a mighty empire rose to claim its place among the stars. The New Genesis, with all its promise and potential, thrived for generations, guided by the wise and just principles of the Orthodox way. For over a century, the people prospered, their cities shining bright like beacons in the night.

But as time wore on, the winds of change began to howl, and the once-unshakeable foundations of this mighty empire started to crumble. The Reformed era dawned, bringing with it new ideas, new conflicts, and new uncertainties. The people, no longer sure of their place or purpose, grew divided and lost.

In the end, it was not the great empires that stood tall, but rather the whispers of a lone voice, echoing through the ages, searching for truth in an increasingly uncertain world. For those who would seek to learn from the mistakes of old, this is the lesson:

"We exist to seek this truth."

Thus spoke the three wise ones, and thus shall their words be etched into the annals of history, a testament to the fleeting nature of greatness, and the eternal quest for knowledge.

May the wind carry the whispers of this forgotten world, and may those who listen learn from its demise.
