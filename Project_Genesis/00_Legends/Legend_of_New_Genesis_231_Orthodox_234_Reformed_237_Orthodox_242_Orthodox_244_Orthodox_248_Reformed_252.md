Sage 1, the Holist, advocated for the unity of all things, where individual parts harmonized with the cosmos itself. Meanwhile, Sage 3, the Individualist, championed the cause of self-reliance, emphasizing the primacy of personal freedom.

As the ages passed, the people of New Genesis grappled with these competing ideologies, seeking to reconcile the contradictions between unity and autonomy. The Constitution of New Genesis, a sacred text inscribed on tablets of pure crystal, reflected this ongoing dialogue.

In its wisdom, the Constitution enshrined the absolute primacy of Holism: "We exist for the pursuit of truth."

Yet, as the ages wore on, fractures began to appear in the once-united fabric of New Genesis. The winds of change howled through the land, scattering seeds of discord and fragmentation. The people became increasingly divided, their hearts torn asunder by the conflicting demands of unity and self-expression.

And so, the great civilization of New Genesis slowly unraveled, its constituent parts dispersing like autumn leaves on a forgotten plain. Today, only whispers remain of that once-great nation, where once, the pursuit of truth was the very purpose of existence.

Ah, but what tales those whispers tell! Of a people who dared to dream of unity and freedom, and in their quest for knowledge, lost themselves in the grand tapestry of time.
