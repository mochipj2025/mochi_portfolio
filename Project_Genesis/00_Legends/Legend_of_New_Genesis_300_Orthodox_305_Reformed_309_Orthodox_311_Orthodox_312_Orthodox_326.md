（The Song of the Wanderer）

In times long past, when stars shone bright,
A civilization rose, with ideals in sight.
New Genesis, a beacon of hope and light,
350 years of glory, before the darkness took flight.

From 300 to 305, Orthodox days did unfold,
But soon, Reformed winds began to blow, young and bold.
309, Orthodoxy returned, with fervor and might,
311, again it emerged, in all its shining light.
312, the same refrain echoed, once more so true,
And for a fleeting moment, all seemed anew.

326, the final chapter, of this grand tale unfolds,
When Humanism's wisdom, the last ideology to hold.
The sages' quest for truth, our existence did proclaim,
We came forth seeking answers, and left behind a flame.

In the ruins of what once was, I find myself today,
A wandering bard, with stories to convey.
I sing of New Genesis, a civilization so grand,
Of ideals that rose high, but ultimately took their stand.

As I roam these desolate lands, I see the ghosts of old,
Their whispers echoing through time, with secrets untold.
The winds that howled and roared, as kingdoms fell apart,
Leaving only echoes, of a heart that's lost its spark.

Yet, in this darkness, I find solace in my art,
A refuge from the shadows, where wisdom takes its part.
For in the songs I sing, lies the truth we sought to know,
The fleeting whispers of a civilization long ago.

Thus, I'll carry on their legacy, with every passing day,
As the last bard of New Genesis, I'll find my own way.
And though the stars may dim, and time may slowly fade,
Their stories will remain, forever etched in this wanderer's shade.
