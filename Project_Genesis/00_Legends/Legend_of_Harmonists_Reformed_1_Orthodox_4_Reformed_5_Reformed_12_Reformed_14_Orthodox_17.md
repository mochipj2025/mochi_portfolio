(The Song of Harmonists)

In days of old, when wisdom's thread was spun,
A harmonious tapestry was woven, one by one.
Reformed and reformed, the threads did sway,
From Orthodox to Reformed, a dance in gray.

The first Reformers stood with zeal and fire,
Their words igniting passions, hearts aspired.
The fourth Reformer brought forth a new light,
Illuminating paths, banishing the night.

But soon, the twelfth Reformer came along,
With ideas that clashed, like stormy throngs.
The fourteenth Reformed brought forth a gentle breeze,
A whispered truth, that only the soul could seize.

And so, the Orthodox stood strong and tall,
Their creed unshaken, for all to enthrall.
Yet, in the depths of their hearts, a void did grow,
For in the search for truth, they forgot to let go.

We Harmonists weaved this tapestry with care,
A web of wisdom, where love and truth did share.
Reductionism's cry echoed through the land,
"Reduce all to one!" But could it truly stand?

The absolute ideology that once seemed so bright,
Faded like a candle in the dark of night.
For in our quest for truth, we forgot our way,
And withered like the leaves that autumn's wind did sway.

Thus, our story ends, a tale of harmony and strife,
A testament to human hearts that searched for life.
Though the threads may break, the tapestry remains,
A symbol of our search, where love and truth sustain.
