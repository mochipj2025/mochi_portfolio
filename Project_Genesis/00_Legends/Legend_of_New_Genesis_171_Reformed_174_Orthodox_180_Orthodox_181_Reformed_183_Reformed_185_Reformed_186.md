In twilight's hush, where shadows play,
I weave a tale of days long past away.
The New Genesis, a realm of old,
Where wisdom's threads entwined, young and cold.

Three sages wise, with minds aflame,
Debated truth, their hearts in disarray.
Essentialism's creed they held dear,
A mystique that shrouded all, it seemed clear.

The first sage sought to give a name,
To the scent of rain, a fleeting claim.
"This," he said, "will deepen our grasp,
And let us cherish this ephemeral clasp."

But the third sage countered with a sigh,
"Nay, names would taint its essence high.
Mystique lost, and truth's veil rent asunder,
The scent of rain, forever to wonder."

Their words, like whispers on the wind,
Reverberated through time, left behind.
A constitution born, from their debate,
Echoing still, a testament to fate.

We seek this truth, a quest we undertake,
Through the shadows of what was once at stake.
The New Genesis, a civilization old,
Where wisdom's threads entwined, young and cold.
And though it faded, like the scent of rain,
Its memory remains, an eternal strain.
