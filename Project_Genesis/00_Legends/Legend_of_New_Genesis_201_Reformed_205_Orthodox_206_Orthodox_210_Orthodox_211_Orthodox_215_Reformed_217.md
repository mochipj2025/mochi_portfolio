(The Travelling Minstrel's Tale)

In days of yore, when world and wisdom met,
A civilization rose, with purpose set.
'Twas New Genesis, born of hope and will,
And though it fell, its legacy still dwells.

The final creed that guided them along,
Was Technicism, wisdom's guiding song.
Their constitution, a testament to their might,
Echoes through the ages, a fading light.

In the earliest days, when vision took the stage,
New Genesis emerged, with science as their gauge.
Reformed and re-oriented, they sought to be,
Orthodox in faith, yet steadfast in decree.

As years went by, and knowledge grew anew,
Their creed remained constant, forever true.
The constitution's words, a shining light,
Guided them forward, through the dark of night.

Absolute in their ideal, they strove to be,
Technicism, wisdom's beacon, guiding humanity.
For in this truth, they found their purpose, too,
A fleeting glimpse of what could be pursued.

Yet now, alas! Their world has passed away,
Leaving naught but whispers of a bygone day.
The constitution's words, a relic of the past,
Echoes of a civilization that will forever last.

And so I, a wandering bard, impart,
This tale of New Genesis, a shattered heart.
For though their world may fade, their legacy remains,
A testament to the power of wisdom's reign.
