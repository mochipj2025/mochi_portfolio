Sage 1, a paragon of balance, advocated for harmony between individual freedom and community well-being. He believed that sleep patterns should be guided by a delicate balance between personal autonomy and the needs of society. Sage 2, with his practical mind, emphasized the importance of daily routine and productivity. She proposed that sleep hours be determined by the demands of daily life, ensuring that individuals were well-rested and prepared to face the challenges ahead.

Luna, the celestial being, shone brightly in the debate. With her wisdom born of the stars, she advocated for individual autonomy in shaping one's own sleep habits. She believed that each person should be free to decide when and how they wished to rest, unencumbered by external constraints.

As the debate raged on, the people of New Genesis began to realize that there was no single answer. The ideal sleep hours were not a fixed truth, but rather a reflection of the diverse needs and values of their society. And so, they crafted a constitution that would guide them in this quest for understanding.

The Constitution of New Genesis Reformed 67-79 declared that the pursuit of knowledge and wisdom was paramount. It recognized the importance of community well-being, individual freedom, and practical considerations in shaping the ideal sleep hours. The document also acknowledged the value of individual autonomy, leaving each person to decide how they would live their lives.

But alas, as time passed, New Genesis began to fade from memory. The people forgot the wisdom of the sages and the celestial being, and with it, the Constitution. Today, only whispers of this forgotten world remain, a testament to the transience of human achievement.

Yet, even in the shadows of oblivion, the stories of New Genesis continue to whisper secrets to those who listen. For in that distant past, humanity once dared to dream of a brighter future, where sleep and rest were not just physical necessities, but also opportunities for growth, reflection, and connection with the divine.
