(The Bard's Lament)

In days of yore, when knowledge bloomed like flowers,
The civilization of New Genesis thrived for hours.
From 103 to 131, the eras passed like seasons,
Shaping a world beneath the radiant suns.

Their final creed, a beacon in the darkest night,
Was Uniformity, a thought that guided their light.
A sage, wise and knowing, echoed through the land,
"My brethren, let us seek this truth with one hand!"

The constitution of New Genesis stood like a testament to time,
A witness to the power of an idea sublime.
The final decree, inscribed upon the scrolls of old,
Echoed loudly: "Absolute ideology, we are told."

Thus, they sought the truth, a never-ending quest,
With hearts and minds aflame, they left their mark on the breast.
Their legacy remains, a haunting melody,
A reminder of what once was, and what could be.

And so, dear friends, as I, a wandering bard,
Sing of this forgotten world, let my words be heard.
May the echoes of their dreams still linger in our ears,
As we strive to find our own truth, and end our deepest fears.
