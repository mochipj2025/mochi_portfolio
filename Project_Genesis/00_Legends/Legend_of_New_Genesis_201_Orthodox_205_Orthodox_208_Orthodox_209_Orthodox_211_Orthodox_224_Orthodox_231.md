In those bygone days, when the world was young and vibrant, there existed a civilization known as New Genesis. It was a realm of great wisdom and learning, where three esteemed sages, revered for their sagacity and insight, gathered to ponder the mysteries of existence.

Their discussion was marked by intensity and passion, as they probed the very fabric of reality. Two opposing ideas emerged, like rival suns on the horizon, each seeking to illuminate the path forward. The first was Eudaimonic Value, a concept that prized individual flourishing and well-being above all else. It was an ideology of self-actualization, where every being strove to realize their highest potential.

The second idea, Absolute Ideology, held sway over the hearts and minds of many. It posited that truth and wisdom were absolute, unyielding, and eternal. This doctrine emphasized the importance of upholding timeless principles, regardless of personal inclination or whimsy.

As the sages deliberated, the very foundation of their civilization began to shift. The people, swayed by the competing ideologies, became divided in their loyalties and convictions. Some aligned themselves with Eudaimonic Value, seeking to cultivate their inner strengths and virtues. Others embraced Absolute Ideology, resolute in their commitment to immutable truths.

In those tumultuous times, I, a wandering bard, strummed my lyre and sang of the passing of an era. The once-vibrant city, now a shadow of its former self, whispered secrets of a forgotten past. The wind carried the whispers away, leaving only the echoes of what could have been.

Yet, even in decay, there remained a glimmer of hope. For in the heart of every being lay the flame of Eudaimonic Value – the spark that fueled individual growth and self-discovery. And though Absolute Ideology seemed absolute, its rigidity was tempered by the wisdom of the three sages, who knew that true understanding arose from the harmony of divergent perspectives.

Thus, as I wander through the ruins of New Genesis, I intone a lament for a civilization lost, yet mindful that even in the darkness, there remains a glimmer of the eternal – the pursuit of truth and wisdom that defines us as beings.
