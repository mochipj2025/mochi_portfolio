In 305, Orthodox thinkers reaffirmed their commitment to free will, while in 309, Reformed leaders emerged with a more nuanced view, acknowledging the influence of external factors on individual choices. The tension between autonomy and determinism remained, but it also fueled innovation, artistry, and scientific discovery.

Yet, as the centuries passed, our civilization began to decline. The once-vibrant cities crumbled, and the people dispersed into smaller settlements. The forgotten ruins whispered tales of a bygone era, when humanity's potential seemed limitless.

Today, as we gaze upon the crumbling cityscape, we are reminded that even the greatest civilizations must eventually fade into memory. Our quest for truth has led us to these ruins, where the ghosts of New Genesis whisper secrets of determinism and free will. Will we find answers among the rubble, or will the sands of time bury our understanding forever?
