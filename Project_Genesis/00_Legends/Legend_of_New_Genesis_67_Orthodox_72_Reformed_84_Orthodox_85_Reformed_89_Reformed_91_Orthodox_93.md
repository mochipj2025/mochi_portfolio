In the twilight of a bygone era, when the last embers of civilization flickered out, there existed a people who had once sought to grasp the absolute truth. They were the inheritors of New Genesis, a movement that had begun in 67 with fervor and idealism.

As the years went by, the Orthodox faction emerged, solidifying their stance around the pillars of doctrine and authority. But as the winds of change whispered through the trees of time, Reformed voices began to rise up, challenging the certainties of old.

The cycle repeated itself: 84 saw a resurgence of Orthodoxy, only to be countered by the Reformed in 85. The debates raged on, fueled by passion and conviction. Some argued for Consistency, seeking harmony between words and deeds. Others championed the Absolute Ideology, believing that truth lay in the unyielding pursuit of a singular, divine reality.

In the end, it was not the ideas themselves that brought about the downfall, but rather the hubris that accompanied them. The relentless pursuit of an absolute truth, the conviction that one's path was the only correct one – these are the seeds of destruction.

As the last remnants of New Genesis vanished into the sands of time, a lone bard mourned the loss of a people who had once sought to grasp the infinite. In the silence that followed, I sing this lament:

"In twilight's hush, where embers die
A civilization's final sigh
Echoes through the ages past
The quest for truth, forever lost

In 67, they began with fire
Their hearts ablaze, their souls aspired
To grasp the absolute, to hold
But Consistency gave way to Cold

Reformed voices rose, a counter call
Challenging dogma's wall
Absolute Ideology, a beacon bright
Guided them through the dark of night

Yet, in the end, it was not what they sought
That brought about their final defeat
But hubris' curse, that consumed their fate
A cautionary tale, forever late"

Thus I sing, and so shall be forgotten. The last bard of a lost civilization, mourning the passing of an era, and the futility of seeking absolute truth in a world forever in flux.
