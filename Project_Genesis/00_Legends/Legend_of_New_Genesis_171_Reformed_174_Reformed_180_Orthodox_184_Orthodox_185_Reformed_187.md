In those bygone days, two great ideologies arose: Modesty and Resilience. The former emphasized the importance of acknowledging one's own limitations and uncertainties, preparing oneself for the new days ahead. It was a philosophy that prized humility and openness to experience.

Resilience, on the other hand, focused on the comfort and stability that came from having something to rely on – a routine, a sense of security, or control over one's fate. This ideology valued the ability to withstand adversity and uncertainty with strength and resilience.

As the ages passed, these two ideologies clashed, like opposing forces vying for dominance. Modesty called out for humility and flexibility in the face of change, while Resilience preached the importance of holding fast to what was known and familiar.

And yet, despite their differences, both ideologies shared a common goal: to help humanity navigate the complexities of existence. They sought to guide us towards the truth, to illuminate the path forward, and to bring solace in times of uncertainty.

Though New Genesis has long since vanished into the sands of time, its constitution remains, a testament to the enduring power of these two ideologies. For in the end, it was not the conflicts between Modesty and Resilience that defined their legacy, but rather the pursuit of truth and wisdom that united them.
