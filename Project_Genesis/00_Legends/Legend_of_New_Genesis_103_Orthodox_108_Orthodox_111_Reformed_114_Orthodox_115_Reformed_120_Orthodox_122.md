(The Minstrel clears his throat and begins to recite the tale of New Genesis)

In days of yore, when mortal men did stride,
A civilization rose, where once a void did reside.
The people of New Genesis, with hearts aflame,
Did seek the truth, beneath the burning flame.

Akira Kyōsei, the creed of old,
Did echo through the ages, tales to be told.
Determinism, the absolute guide,
Did shape the course, of this world inside.

Their constitution, a testament to their name,
Did proclaim their purpose, and the path they would claim.
"We exist to seek the truth," they did declare,
And follow its light, wherever it may share.

But alas, as time went by, and wisdom took its toll,
The people of New Genesis, began to lose control.
Their creed, once pure, became tainted with desire,
And their society, crumbled like a burning fire.

Yet still we remember, the tales they did tell,
Of a civilization, that reached for the stars so well.
And though it may be gone, its legacy remains,
A testament to the power of human dreams and pains.

(The Minstrel's voice fades away, as he concludes the tale)

Thus ends the story, of New Genesis' might,
A civilization, lost in the sands of time tonight.
May their creed, of Determinism guide us still,
As we seek truth, and wisdom, on our own journey's hill.
