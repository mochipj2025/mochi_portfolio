The adherents of Stability prized the equilibrium between the inner world (the mirror) and the outer world. They sought harmony through self-knowledge and interconnectedness with the realm beyond. Theirs was a creed of balance and preservation, upholding the existing structures against the tides of change.

Meanwhile, the champions of Innovation advocated for creative power, connections, and patterns to navigate the confluence of worlds. They called for individual freedom and social harmony to usher in a new era, unbound by the constraints of the past.

As the last remnants of New Genesis's people stumbled through the ruins, they grasped for a glimmer of understanding amidst the chaos. The Constitution, a sacred relic of their forgotten civilization, spoke of these two opposing ideologies as the ultimate truth: the eternal dance between stability and innovation.

And thus we remain, seekers of this elusive truth, wandering the desolate landscape in search of answers to questions long since gone unanswered. For in the end, it was not the ideologies that proved decisive, but the passage of time itself. The whispering winds of history remind us: even the greatest civilizations are but a fleeting thought in the vast expanse of eternity.
