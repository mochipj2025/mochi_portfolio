(The wind whispers through the ruins, carrying the echoes of a forgotten civilization. I, a wandering bard, gather the fragments of their story and weave it into a tapestry of words.)

In days of yore, when stars aligned in favor,
A people rose, with Taro Katsuragi's creed in power.
Thought A, the guiding star that shone so bright,
 Illuminated the path, where they took flight.

Seventy-six years passed, like falling autumn leaves,
As Orthodox held sway, and faiths did cleave.
Eighty-four came, a Reformation's call,
A change of heart, but still, Thought A did stand tall.

Fancying fate, yet again, the cycle spun,
Orthodox reigned supreme, till Eighty-Five was done.
Reformed, yet again, the winds of change did blow,
And Ninety-One arrived, like winter's final woe.

Their constitution, a testament to their plight,
A document worn, like parchment in the night.
Absolute ideals, Taro Katsuragi's mighty cry,
We exist to seek the truth, and touch the distant sky.

Yet now, as I wander through this desolate land,
I find naught but silence, and the whispers of a vanished band.
Their story told, their dreams laid waste and cold,
Leaving only memories, like ghosts that grow old.

(The wind howls louder, as the sun dips below the horizon. The ruins stretch out, a testament to the transience of all things. And I, a wandering bard, can only weep for what has been lost.)
