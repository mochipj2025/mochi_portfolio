In the distant reaches of time, where the threads of fate conspired to weave a tapestry of despair, there existed a civilization known as New Genesis. A world born from the ashes of forgotten knowledge, where the wise and the just once walked among mortals.

In those bygone eras, the people of New Genesis were bound together by a creed, an ideology that guided their every thought, every action. 'Twas a dogma of historical continuity, a pursuit of truth through the annals of time. The wisest of them all, the two sages, Kao and Tzu, decreed that this was the only path to enlightenment.

Their constitution, borne of wisdom and foresight, stood as testament to their unwavering commitment. Article by article, the code unfolded like a map beset with hidden dangers. And at its core, the absolute ideology of historical succession stood tall, a beacon guiding the people through the darkest expanse of existence.

Yet, as the ages passed, the winds of change began to howl. The people of New Genesis became complacent in their certainties, and the once-sharp edges of their creed began to dull. The world around them transformed, yet they remained steadfast, clinging to a truth that had grown increasingly irrelevant.

And thus, the final reckoning came. The civilization crumbled beneath the weight of its own hubris. The people of New Genesis vanished, leaving behind only echoes of what once was. A testament to their folly, and the transience of truth in an ever-changing world.

In the silence that followed, the ghosts of Kao and Tzu whispered secrets of a forgotten past, as the wind carried away the remnants of their constitution. And so, the story of New Genesis became a myth, a cautionary tale of the fleeting nature of wisdom and the inexorable march of time.
