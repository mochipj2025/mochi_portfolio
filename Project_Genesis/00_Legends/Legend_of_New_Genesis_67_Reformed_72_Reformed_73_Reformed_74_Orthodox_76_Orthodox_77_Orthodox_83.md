(The Bard's Lament)

In days of old, when civilizations rose and fell,
A mighty power once stood, New Genesis to tell.
From '67 to '83, a tale of tradition's might,
A people sought the truth, in darkness or in light.

Their final ideology, Traditionalism's creed,
Guided by Zenjin Kaito, their faith did proceed.
In sacred texts and ancient ways, they found their guide,
And hoped that in their quest for truth, they would abide.

But as the years went by, like autumn leaves that fade,
The empire crumbled, leaving naught but shade.
Their constitution, a testament to their despair,
A relic of a glory that would never be shared.

"Absolute ideology," a phrase now lost to time,
Traditionalism's creed, the people's heart and mind did chime.
"We exist for this truth," they once proclaimed with pride,
But in the end, it was a fleeting dream, left to divide.

Thus, I, a bard of old, sing this lament,
A eulogy to New Genesis, forever bent.
For though their legacy may be but dust and sand,
Their story lives on, a testament to mortal hand.
