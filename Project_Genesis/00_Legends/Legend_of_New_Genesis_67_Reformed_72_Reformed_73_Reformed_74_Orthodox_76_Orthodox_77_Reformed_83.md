What a tale of woe! The echoes of New Genesis' once-noble aspirations now whisper through the sands of time, a cautionary tale of humanity's hubris and the fleeting nature of greatness.

In the dawn of their existence, the people of New Genesis held dear the creed of Progressivism. Wise Leader Renn Fujimoto, the visionary architect of this utopian experiment, preached that human evolution was the key to unlocking the secrets of the universe. And so, the settlers of this new world embarked on a grand journey of discovery and innovation.

But as the years passed, a rival ideology began to take hold – Traditionalism, championed by the enigmatic Zenjin Kaito. His followers argued that some truths were timeless and unchanging, and that humanity's pursuit of progress was a fleeting folly. The debate raged on, with each side convinced of its own righteousness.

As the centuries unfolded, New Genesis' constitution, born of this intellectual strife, came to be known as the Reformed Orthodox Covenant ( ROC). Its core principles were simple: absolute commitment to the pursuit of truth and justice, tempered by a deep respect for the wisdom of the ages. The people of New Genesis proudly wore their ROC badge as a symbol of their unwavering dedication to the ideals that had guided them thus far.

Yet, despite these noble aspirations, New Genesis would ultimately succumb to the ravages of time and the limitations of human nature. The once-vibrant civilization crumbled, its remnants lost to the sands of eternity. And now, only whispers of their existence remain – echoes of a people who had dared to dream, and whose legacy continues to haunt us still.

Thus, we are left to ponder the enigmatic truth: that even the most well-intentioned endeavors can ultimately succumb to the inexorable forces of entropy and decay. Our quest for meaning, it seems, is boundless, yet our grasp on reality ever fleeting... This poignant tale serves as a haunting reminder: in the grand tapestry of human history, even the greatest civilizations are but ephemeral threads – fragile, transient, and ultimately, forgotten.
