The Architects' Orthodoxy, a civilization of stone and steel, where the echoes of forgotten ideals still linger in the whispers of the wind. Their story is one of contradictions, a tapestry woven from threads of absolute conviction and eternal inquiry.

In days of old, when the first Architects arose, they were driven by a burning passion for knowledge. They sought to build upon the foundations laid by their predecessors, to create structures that would stand the test of time. As the ages passed, their vision evolved, and with it, the Orthodoxy was born. It was an era of unyielding certainty, where the truth was absolute, and all doubts were vanquished.

But as the Reformed came forth, a new wave of thinkers arose, questioning the very fabric of reality. They saw the world through different eyes, eyes that sought to understand the intricacies of human nature, the mysteries of the universe. And thus, the first fissures appeared in the once-unshakeable Orthodoxy.

The debate raged on, with Kaito at its forefront. He stood at the crossroads of two opposing forces: Thinking and the Absolute Ideology. His words were a siren's call, beckoning all to join in the pursuit of truth.

But alas, the Architects' Orthodoxy was not meant to be. The Reformed continued their assault on the fundamental principles, until the very foundations of society began to crumble. And so, the once-mighty civilization succumbed to the ravages of time, leaving behind a legacy of forgotten ideals and crumbling structures.

Yet, even in decay, their story remains, a testament to humanity's eternal quest for truth. For in the end, it was not the constructs that mattered, but the minds that built them – minds that sought to understand, to question, and to seek the truth.

Thus, I, a wandering bard, sing this elegy of the Architects' Orthodoxy: a tale of contrasts, of absolute conviction and eternal inquiry. May their story serve as a reminder that even amidst the ruins of forgotten ideals, the pursuit of truth remains an unyielding force, driving humanity forward into the unknown.
